In setting forth the idea that the crucifixion was a "stumblingblock" to the Jews, Paul used a metaphor common to both Greeks and Hebrews. The word in the original Greek is skandalon, the same from which we derive our word scandal The skandalon was the movable trigger-stick of a trap or snare which, when struck by the foot, caused the striker to be caught by the trap. It is often used in the New Testament as a symbol of Christ because his appearance and brief sojourn among men was so different from that which the Jews anticipated. They expected a mighty king of glory who would throw off the hated yoke of

Rome in one miraculous stroke and establish a messianic kingdom in which faithful Jews would reign supreme.
