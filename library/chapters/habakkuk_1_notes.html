Donaldson Commentary●●●○○Cross-ref · Nahum 3:19●●●○○
God's answer to Habakkuk's first question posed another question in Habakkuk's mind, to which he asked an answer of God. Habakkuk differs from other of the prophets in that his book is mainly a dialogue between himself and the Lord. He inquired of God, he asked, and he had the faith to receive an answer. Habakkuk asked God the questions and then said: "I will stand upon my watch, and set me upon the tower, and will watch to see what he will say unto me . . . " (2: 1 ).
