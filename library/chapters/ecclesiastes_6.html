1 THERE is an <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.407">evil which I have seen under the sun, and it [is] common among men:
2 A man to whom <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.908">God hath given riches, wealth, and honour, so that he wanteth nothing for his soul of all that he desireth, yet God giveth him not power to eat thereof, but a stranger eateth it: this [is] vanity, and it [is] an evil disease.

6 Yea, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.926">though he live a thousand years twice [told], yet hath he seen no good: do not all go to one place?
7 All the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.917">labour of man [is] for his mouth, and yet the appetite is not filled.
8 For what hath the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.892">wise more than the fool? what hath the poor, that knoweth to walk before the living?
9 Better [is] the sight of the eyes than the wandering of the desire: this [is] also <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">vanity and vexation of spirit.

11 Seeing there be many things that increase vanity, what [is] man the better?
12 For who <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.995">knoweth what [is] good for man in [this] life, all the days of his vain life which he spendeth as a shadow? for who can tell a man what shall be after him under the sun?
