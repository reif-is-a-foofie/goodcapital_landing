Doctrine and Covenants

1 In the celestial <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">glory there are three heavens or degrees;
2 And in order to obtain the highest, a man must enter into this order of the priesthood [meaning the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">new and everlasting covenant of marriage];
3 And if he does not, he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_genesis_1_260.265">cannot obtain it.
4 He may enter into the other, but that is the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">end of his kingdom; he cannot have an increase.
5 (May 17th, 1843.) The more <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">sure word of prophecy means a man's knowing that he is sealed up unto eternal life, by revelation and the spirit of prophecy, through the power of the Holy Priesthood.
6 It is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.389">impossible for a man to be saved in ignorance.
7 There is no such thing as immaterial matter. All <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">spirit is matter, but it is more fine or pure, and can only be discerned by purer eyes;
8 We <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">cannot see it; but when our bodies are purified we shall see that it is all matter.
