The doctrine of repentance is much broader than a dictionary's definition. When Jesus said "repent," His disciples recorded that command in the Greek language with the verb metanoeo. This powerful word has great significance. In this word, the prefix meta means "change." The suffix relates to four important Greek terms: nous, meaning "the mind"; gnosis, meaning "knowledge"; pneuma, meaning "spirit"; and pnoe, meaning "breath."
