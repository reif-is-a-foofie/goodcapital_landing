Types of the Second Coming in Third Nephi by Lee Donaldson Sacred scripture both describes actual events and foreshadows future realities. The Savior clearly taught this dual nature of scripture to the Nephites when he commanded them to search the writings of Isaiah, because this ancient prophet "spake as touching all things concerning my people which are of the house of Israel . . . And all things that he spake have been and shall be , even according to the words which he spake " (3 Nephi 23:1-3; emphasis added). This manner of prophesying is called typology. Typology is a divine means to prepare the minds of God's people for the future. It testifies that God's hand is in history and he directs the affairs of his children.

For example, Abraham's sacrifice of Isaac was the type and Heavenly Father's sacrifice of his Only Begotten Son was the antitype. Paul noted that Melchizedek was a type of Christ. Melchizedek, like Christ, was a priest and king whose parentage is not tied to mortal man (Hebrews 7:1- 8:1). Bruce Jorgenson points out a vital underpinning of typology. "Figural [typological] interpretation is not figurative, not merely allegorical, for both figure and fulfillment, both type and antitype, or in Auerbach's word, both 'sign and what it signifies' are regarded as fully historical.
