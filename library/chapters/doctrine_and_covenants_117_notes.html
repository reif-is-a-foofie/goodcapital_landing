The Least of These By President Boyd K. Packer Acting President of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles Let no one underestimate the power of faith in the ordinary Latter-day Saints. There is a message for Latter-day Saints in a seldom quoted revelation given to the Prophet Joseph Smith in 1838. “I remember my servant Oliver Granger; behold, verily I say unto him that his name shall be had in sacred remembrance from generation to generation, forever and ever, saith the Lord” ( D&amp;C 117:12 ). Oliver Granger was a very ordinary man.
Donaldson Commentary●●●○○Cross-ref · Moroni 10:34●●●○○
My study recently brought me to an acquaintance with Oliver Granger. I was not aware of this person in Church history. Yet, Doctrine and Covenants 117:12 states, "I remember my servant Oliver Granger; behold, verily I say unto him that his name shall be had in sacred remembrance." A little research disclosed that Oliver Granger was a man of considerable business acumen. He was one of the Prophet Joseph's business agents. He was planning to leave Kirtland, but the Prophet Joseph left him to handle the business affairs of the First Presidency.
