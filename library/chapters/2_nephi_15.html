2 Nephi

15

1 AND then will I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_2_nephi_15_10.607">sing to my well-beloved a song of my beloved, touching his vineyard. My well-beloved hath a vineyard in a very fruitful hill.
2 And he fenced it, and gathered out the stones thereof, and planted it with the choicest <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.620">vine, and built a tower in the midst of it, and also made a wine-press therein; and he looked that it should bring forth grapes, and it brought forth wild grapes.
3 And now, O <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.391">inhabitants of Jerusalem, and men of Judah, judge, I pray you, betwixt me and my vineyard.
4 What could have been done more to my <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_2_nephi_15_40.979">vineyard that I have not done in it? Wherefore, when I looked that it should bring forth grapes it brought forth wild grapes.
5 And now go to; I will tell <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">you what I will do to my vineyard--I will take away the hedge thereof, and it shall be eaten up; and I will break down the wall thereof, and it shall be trodden down;
6 And I will <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">lay it waste; it shall not be pruned nor digged; but there shall come up briers and thorns; I will also command the clouds that they rain no rain upon it.
7 For the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.940">vineyard of the Lord of Hosts is the house of Israel, and the men of Judah his pleasant plant; and he looked for judgment, and behold, oppression; for righteousness, but behold, a cry.
8 Wo unto them that <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.683">join house to house, till there can be no place, that they may be placed alone in the midst of the earth!
9 In <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">mine ears, said the Lord of Hosts, of a truth many houses shall be desolate, and great and fair cities without inhabitant.
10 Yea, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_isaiah_5_100.308">ten acres of vineyard shall yield one bath, and the seed of a homer shall yield an ephah.
11 Wo unto them that <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.810">rise up early in the morning, that they may follow strong drink, that continue until night, and wine inflame them!
12 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.996">harp, and the viol, the tabret, and pipe, and wine are in their feasts; but they regard not the work of the Lord, neither consider the operation of his hands.
13 Therefore, my people are <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.830">gone into captivity, because they have no knowledge; and their honorable men are famished, and their multitude dried up with thirst.
14 Therefore, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.995">hell hath enlarged herself, and opened her mouth without measure; and their glory, and their multitude, and their pomp, and he that rejoiceth, shall descend into it.
15 And the mean man shall be brought down, and the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.958">mighty man shall be humbled, and the eyes of the lofty shall be humbled.
16 But the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.953">Lord of Hosts shall be exalted in judgment, and God that is holy shall be sanctified in righteousness.
17 Then shall the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.968">lambs feed after their manner, and the waste places of the fat ones shall strangers eat.
18 Wo unto them that <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.871">draw iniquity with cords of vanity, and sin as it were with a cart rope;
19 That <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.944">say: Let him make speed, hasten his work, that we may see it; and let the counsel of the Holy One of Israel draw nigh and come, that we may know it.
20 Wo unto them that <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.616">call evil good, and good evil, that put darkness for light, and light for darkness, that put bitter for sweet, and sweet for bitter!
21 Wo unto the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_2_nephi_15_210.756">wise in their own eyes and prudent in their own sight!
22 Wo unto the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.865">mighty to drink wine, and men of strength to mingle strong drink;
23 Who <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.986">justify the wicked for reward, and take away the righteousness of the righteous from him!
24 Therefore, as the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.927">fire devoureth the stubble, and the flame consumeth the chaff, their root shall be rottenness, and their blossoms shall go up as dust; because they have cast away the law of the Lord of Hosts, and despised the word of the Holy One of Israel.
25 Therefore, is the anger of the Lord kindled against his people, and he hath <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.973">stretched forth his hand against them, and hath smitten them; and the hills did tremble, and their carcasses were torn in the midst of the streets. For all this his anger is not turned away, but his hand is stretched out still.
26 And he will <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.754">lift up an ensign to the nations from far, and will hiss unto them from the end of the earth; and behold, they shall come with speed swiftly; none shall be weary nor stumble among them.
27 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.830">None shall slumber nor sleep; neither shall the girdle of their loins be loosed, nor the latchet of their shoes be broken;
28 Whose arrows shall be sharp, and all their bows bent, and their <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.936">horses' hoofs shall be counted like flint, and their wheels like a whirlwind, their roaring like a lion.
29 They shall roar like young lions; yea, they shall roar, and lay hold of the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.879">prey, and shall carry away safe, and none shall deliver.
30 And in that day they shall <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.968">roar against them like the roaring of the sea; and if they look unto the land, behold, darkness and sorrow, and the light is darkened in the heavens thereof.
