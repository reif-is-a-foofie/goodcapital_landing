Donaldson Commentary

In ancient times, the first day of each month was a holiday (Numbers 10:10; 28:11) and provided a convenient opportunity to bring the people together for important announcements (Numbers 9:1; Deuteronomy 1:3; Ezekiel 26:1), as noted by Ehrlich. (Milgrom, JPS Numbers, 4)

"The tent was regarded as a portable Sinai: As Moses was permitted to ascend to the presence of God atop the mountain (Exodus 19:20; 34:2) so might he enter the Holy of Holies …; as Moses was accompanied by priest and elders only until he reached the cloud perimeter of Mount Sinai (Exodus 24:1-2, 9, 15), so the priests could enter the Tent only up to the veil tht sealed off God's beclouded

Presence (Exodus 28:43; 30:20; Leviticus 10:9; 16:2); as the Israelites gathered on the lower slopes of Mount Sinai (Exodus 19:7) … so were they given access to the 'entrance of the Tent'" (Milgrom, Jewish Publication Society Book of Numbers, 4)

2Take ye the sum of all the congregation of the children of Israel, after their families, by the house of their fathers, with the number of [their] names, every male by their polls;
Donaldson Commentary

There are some interesting challenges with this census. 1) It will take only twenty days (cf. Numbers 1:1 with 10:11). King David's census took twenty and nine months (2 Samuel 24:8). 2) Each man's name was to be taken for military purposes, which given the numbers, should take longer than twenty days. A possible solution is how the word 'eleph (Pla) is translated. Jacob Milgrom notes, "since the term 'eleph, thousand," can also mean "clan," it refers in the census to the military forces of the clan, and the hundreds digits in the tribal census refer to the number of contingents subject to conscription. According to this system, Israel's fighting force would have totaled 598 contingents numbering 5,500 men. This latter figure is entirely compatible with the size of armies in the ancient Near East. For example, the armies cited in the Mari archives number 6,000 and 10,000 men (ARM 1.23.42; 6:33,65.) Thus the tribe of Reuben, allegedly totally 46,5000, actually mustered forty-six contingents numbering 500 men (Numbers 1:21), allowing ten or eleven men per unit. (Milgrom, JPS Numbers, 339).

This totals 603,550 men over the age of twenty.
