Defeat of the Canaanite Confederacy - The Invasion of the South.

In this chapter we read of an alliance of five Canaanite kings against the Gibeonites, and of the Gibeonites applying to Joshua for assistance, in virtue of their treaty rights, which Joshua granted. There follows the slaughter of the Canaanite armies by the forces of Israel, chiefly by hailstones from heaven, and of the standing still of the sun and of the moon, while vengeance was taken on them, and of how

five kings hid in a cave, and of what was done to them when taken. This is followed by the taking of Makkedah, Libnah, Lachish, Eglon, Hebron, and Debir, which completed the conquest of the southern part of hill country.
