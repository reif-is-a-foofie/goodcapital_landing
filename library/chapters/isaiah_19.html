Donaldson●○○○○

This brief prophecy concerning an event that occurred in Isaiah's lifetime adds detail to the burden of Egypt (Isa. 20:1; 19:1-15). When King Sargon of Assyria sent his military leader, Tartan, to take the former Philistine city of Ashdod, Isaiah dressed like a slave for three years to warn Israel that if Egypt and Ethiopia were unable to defend themselves against Assyrian enslavement, they certainly could not help protect Judah (Isa. 20:2). Within ten years Assyria indeed invaded Judah and besieged Jerusalem. Isaiah gave a detailed account later of how King Hezekiah of Judah did indeed depend upon the Lord and not upon "this bruised reed, even upon Egypt" (2 Kgs. 18:21; Isa. 20:6). (Ellis T. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament, 516.)
