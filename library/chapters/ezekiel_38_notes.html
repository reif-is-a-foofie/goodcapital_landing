The Gog and Magog prophecy of Ezek. 38-39 is paralleled by John's vision in Rev. 17-20, part of which speaks about the great "whore," the "mother of harlots" (Rev. 17:1-6, 15-18) which falls (Rev. 18:1-3) and whose "smoke rose up for ever and ever" (Rev. 19:1-3). Ancient Name Modern Equivalent Notes Magog (v. 2)

The areas north and east of the Black Sea and the Crimea, territories now occupied by Russia or her former satellites

Magog was inhabited anciently by the Scythians, a fierce barbaric people who were masters of swift and brutal warfare. Thus Magog becomes a symbol, or type, suggesting the warlike nature of these people. Meshech (v. 2)

The people of Meshech were anciently called the Mushki. Some linguists think they gave their name to Moscow (Russian Moskva). This area was also in the territory now under the control of the Russia.

Both Meshech and Tubal, like Magog, were descendants of Japheth (see Genesis 10:2), and the three are usually associated. They seem to have had close geographical and other ties.

By association this area is assumed to have been close to Meshech, very likely in the area around the Black Sea in Georgia and Turkey.

Considering that Gog is to be from Magog, Meshech and Tubal lends credence to the idea that Gog is not necessarily one person but may be a nation or group of leaders who act as one. Persia (v. 5)

Iran In Ezekiel's day Persian had not yet become a great world empire, so its territories were not as extensive at this time as they would be later. Ethiopia (v. 5)

The southern part of the continent of Africa In Hebrew Cush usually represented the whole of Africa lying south of Egypt and the north African coastal countries. Libya (v. 5) The northern African countries Libya (Phut in Hebrew) generally meant North Africa except Egypt.
