Donaldson Commentary

This psalm was probably penned on the occasion of Absalom's rebellion, as Psalm 3rd, 42nd, 43rd; or of Sheba's conspiracy, 2 Sam. 20.

Psalm 71, a prayer for deliverance structured much like Psalm 56, opens with a plea for help and closes with a matching vow to praise God for the help he has given. The psalmist's trust in God is highlighted by its repetition in the second and next-to-last stanzas; and the structurally emphasized central unit represents the main point of the psalm: the psalmist's plea for help (David A. Dorsey, The Literary Structure of the Old Testament)
