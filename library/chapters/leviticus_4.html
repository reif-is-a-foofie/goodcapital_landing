Donaldson●●●●●

Remedies were given for sins committed in ignorance of the law, whether by a priest (Lev. 4:3), a whole congregation (Lev. 4:13), a ruler (Lev. 4:22), or one of the people (Lev. 4:27). The offering was to be made whenever the sin became known. In any such case the priest was to offer the sacrifice on behalf of the guilty party to provide atonement and forgiveness. Ignorance of the law was no excuse, but penalties for breaking the law unwittingly were not as great as those for willful sinning. (Ellis T. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament)
