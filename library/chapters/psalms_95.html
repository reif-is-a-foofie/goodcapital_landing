Donaldson Commentary●●●○○Cross-ref · Ephesians 3:14●●●○○
Knee, Kneeling. 1) Kneeling, as a posture of prayer, was known and practiced during the biblical days (2 Chr. 6:13; Luke 22:4 l; Acts 7:60; Eph. 3:14). For example, three times a day the prophet Daniel "kneeled upon his knees... and prayed, and gave thanks before his God" (Dan. 6:10). The Psalmist also made a connection between kneeling and divine worship when he wrote, "O come, let us worship and bow down: let us kneel before the Lord our maker" (Ps. 95:6). "Kneeling the knees," therefore, signifies an attitude of reverence and worship belonging to the communicant.
