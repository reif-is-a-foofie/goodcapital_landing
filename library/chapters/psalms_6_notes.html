Seven-part symmetric schemes, so common elsewhere in the Hebrew Bible, are relatively scarce in the Psalter. There are, however, a few clear examples of this configuration. Like three-part and five-part chiasmuses, seven-part symmetries allow for emphasis through matched repetition and through the highlighted central position. The symmetric structure of Psalm 7 underscores the contrast between the psalmist's innocence and his enemies' guilt. and highlights Yahweh's role as righteous judge. The psalm's center is the thematic center of the psalm: the psalmist's appeal to Yahweh to vindicate him and condemn his wicked enemies (David A. Dorsey, The Literary Structure of the Old Testament)
