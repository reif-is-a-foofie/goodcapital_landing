1 NOW in the twelfth <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.732">month, that [is], the month Adar, on the thirteenth day of the same, when the king's commandment and his decree drew near to be put in execution, in the day that the enemies of the Jews hoped to have power over them, (though it was turned to the contrary, that the Jews had rule over them that hated them;)
2 The Jews gathered themselves together in their cities throughout all the provinces of the king Ahasuerus, to lay hand on such as sought their <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.629">hurt: and no man could withstand them; for the fear of them fell upon all people.
3 And all the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">rulers of the provinces, and the lieutenants, and the deputies, and officers of the king, helped the Jews; because the fear of Mordecai fell upon them.
4 For <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">Mordecai [was] great in the king's house, and his fame went out throughout all the provinces: for this man Mordecai waxed greater and greater.
5 Thus the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">Jews smote all their enemies with the stroke of the sword, and slaughter, and destruction, and did what they would unto those that hated them.
6 And in <span class="cw" data-tgt="psefariacommentary__ibn_ezra_on_genesis_14_1_10.276">Shushan the palace the Jews slew and destroyed five hundred men.
7 And Parshandatha, and Dalphon, and Aspatha,
8 And Poratha, and Adalia, and Aridatha,
9 And Parmashta, and Arisai, and Aridai, and Vajezatha,
10 The ten sons of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.865">Haman the son of Hammedatha, the enemy of the Jews, slew they; but on the spoil laid they not their hand.
11 On that day the number of those that were <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.944">slain in Shushan the palace was brought before the king.
12 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.983">king said unto Esther the queen, The Jews have slain and destroyed five hundred men in Shushan the palace, and the ten sons of Haman; what have they done in the rest of the king's provinces? now what [is] thy petition? and it shall be granted thee: or what [is] thy request further? and it shall be done.
13 Then said Esther, If it <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.980">please the king, let it be granted to the Jews which [are] in Shushan to do to morrow also according unto this day's decree, and let Haman's ten sons be hanged upon the gallows.
14 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.955">king commanded it so to be done: and the decree was given at Shushan; and they hanged Haman's ten sons.
15 For the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.603">Jews that [were] in Shushan gathered themselves together on the fourteenth day also
16 But the other <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.934">Jews that [were] in the king's provinces gathered themselves together, and stood for their lives, and had rest from their enemies, and slew of their foes seventy and five thousand, but they laid not their hands on the prey,
17 On the thirteenth day of the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.994">month Adar; and on the fourteenth day of the same rested they, and made it a day of feasting and gladness.
18 But the Jews that [were] at Shushan assembled together on the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.942">thirteenth [day] thereof, and on the fourteenth thereof; and on the fifteenth [day] of the same they rested, and made it a day of feasting and gladness.
19 Therefore the Jews of the villages, that dwelt in the unwalled towns, made the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.973">fourteenth day of the month Adar [a day of] gladness and feasting, and a good day, and of sending portions one to another.
20 And Mordecai wrote these things, and sent <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.938">letters unto all the Jews that [were] in all the provinces of the king Ahasuerus, [both] nigh and far,
21 To stablish [this] among them, that they should keep the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.926">fourteenth day of the month Adar, and the fifteenth day of the same, yearly,
22 As the days <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.978">wherein the Jews rested from their enemies, and the month which was turned unto them from sorrow to joy, and from mourning into a good day: that they should make them days of feasting and joy, and of sending portions one to another, and gifts to the poor.
23 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.881">Jews undertook to do as they had begun, and as Mordecai had written unto them;
24 Because <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.881">Haman the son of Hammedatha, the Agagite, the enemy of all the Jews, had devised against the Jews to destroy them, and had cast Pur, that [is], the lot, to consume them, and to destroy them;
25 But when [Esther] came before the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.995">king, he commanded by letters that his wicked device, which he devised against the Jews, should return upon his own head, and that he and his sons should be hanged on the gallows.
26 Wherefore they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.663">called these days Purim after the name of Pur. Therefore for all the words of this letter, and [of that] which they had seen concerning this matter, and which had come unto them,
27 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.893">Jews ordained, and took upon them, and upon their seed, and upon all such as joined themselves unto them, so as it should not fail, that they would keep these two days according to their writing, and according to their [appointed] time every year;
28 And [that] these days [should be] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.916">remembered and kept throughout every generation, every family, every province, and every city; and [that] these days of Purim should not fail from among the Jews, nor the memorial of them perish from their seed.
29 Then Esther the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.878">queen, the daughter of Abihail, and Mordecai the Jew, wrote with all authority, to confirm this second letter of Purim.
30 And he sent the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.946">letters unto all the Jews, to the hundred twenty and seven provinces of the kingdom of Ahasuerus, [with] words of peace and truth,
31 To confirm these days of Purim in their times [appointed], according as <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.918">Mordecai the Jew and Esther the queen had enjoined them, and as they had decreed for themselves and for their seed, the matters of the fastings and their cry.
32 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.872">decree of Esther confirmed these matters of Purim; and it was written in the book.
