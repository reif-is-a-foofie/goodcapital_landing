4How shall we sing the LORD'S song in a strange land?
General Conference
(Joseph Smith, Letter to Hezekiah Peck, from Kirtland, Ohio, 31 August 1835; PWJS, 347.) The Psalmist asked the question, “How shall we sing the Lord’s song in a strange land?” (Psalm 137:4). In every age, including our own, the Lord’s message has been brought into lands that were strange to the messengers. They have sought to establish a Zion where the Lord is God. — James E. Faust, in Conference Report, Oct. 1979, 133; or Ensign, Nov. 1979, 91
Commentary
When the children of Zion are strangers in a strange land, their harps must be hung upon the willows. And they cannot sing the songs of Zion but should mourn and not dance. Therefore, brethren, it remains for all such to be exercised with prayer and continual supplication, until Zion is redeemed. — Joseph Smith, Letter to Hezekiah Peck, from Kirtland, Ohio, 31 August 1835; PWJS, 347.
