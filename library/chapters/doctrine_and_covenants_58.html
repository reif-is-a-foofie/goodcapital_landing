Doctrine and Covenants

58

1 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Hearken, O ye elders of my church, and give ear to my word, and learn of me what I will concerning you, and also concerning this land unto which I have sent you.
2 For verily I say unto <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_11.000">you, blessed is he that keepeth my commandments, whether in life or in death; and he that is faithful in tribulation, the reward of the same is greater in the kingdom of heaven.
3 Ye <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">cannot behold with your natural eyes, for the present time, the design of your God concerning those things which shall come hereafter, and the glory which shall follow after much tribulation.
4 For after much tribulation come the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">blessings. Wherefore the day cometh that ye shall be crowned with much glory; the hour is not yet, but is nigh at hand.
5 Remember this, which I tell <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_11.000">you before, that you may lay it to heart, and receive that which is to follow.
6 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Behold, verily I say unto you, for this cause I have sent you—that you might be obedient, and that your hearts might be prepared to bear testimony of the things which are to come;
7 And also that you might be <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">honored in laying the foundation, and in bearing record of the land upon which the Zion of God shall stand;
8 And also that a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">feast of fat things might be prepared for the poor; yea, a feast of fat things, of wine on the lees well refined, that the earth may know that the mouths of the prophets shall not fail;
9 Yea, a supper of the <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_11.000">house of the Lord, well prepared, unto which all nations shall be invited.
10 First, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.258">rich and the learned, the wise and the noble;
11 And after that <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.965">cometh the day of my power; then shall the poor, the lame, and the blind, and the deaf, come in unto the marriage of the Lamb, and partake of the supper of the Lord, prepared for the great day to come.
12 <span class="cw" data-tgt="psefariacommentary__steinsaltz_on_genesis_24_510.317">Behold, I, the Lord, have spoken it.
13 And that the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.928">testimony might go forth from Zion, yea, from the mouth of the city of the heritage of God—
14 Yea, for this cause I have sent <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.953">you hither, and have selected my servant Edward Partridge, and have appointed unto him his mission in this land.
15 But if he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.932">repent not of his sins, which are unbelief and blindness of heart, let him take heed lest he fall.
16 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.893">Behold his mission is given unto him, and it shall not be given again.
17 And whoso standeth in this mission is appointed to be a <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.958">judge in Israel, like as it was in ancient days, to divide the lands of the heritage of God unto his children;
18 And to <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.941">judge his people by the testimony of the just, and by the assistance of his counselors, according to the laws of the kingdom which are given by the prophets of God.
19 For <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.890">verily I say unto you, my law shall be kept on this land.
20 Let no man <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.973">think he is ruler; but let God rule him that judgeth, according to the counsel of his own will, or, in other words, him that counseleth or sitteth upon the judgment seat.
21 Let no man <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.958">break the laws of the land, for he that keepeth the laws of God hath no need to break the laws of the land.
22 Wherefore, be <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.947">subject to the powers that be, until he reigns whose right it is to reign, and subdues all enemies under his feet.
23 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.960">Behold, the laws which ye have received from my hand are the laws of the church, and in this light ye shall hold them forth. Behold, here is wisdom.
24 And now, as I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.965">spake concerning my servant Edward Partridge, this land is the land of his residence, and those whom he has appointed for his counselors; and also the land of the residence of him whom I have appointed to keep my storehouse;
25 Wherefore, let them bring their families to this <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.914">land, as they shall counsel between themselves and me.
26 For behold, it is not <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.965">meet that I should command in all things; for he that is compelled in all things, the same is a slothful and not a wise servant; wherefore he receiveth no reward.
27 Verily I say, men should be <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.945">anxiously engaged in a good cause, and do many things of their own free will, and bring to pass much righteousness;
28 For the power is in them, wherein they are agents unto themselves. And inasmuch as men do good they shall in <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.939">nowise lose their reward.
29 But he that <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.945">doeth not anything until he is commanded, and receiveth a commandment with doubtful heart, and keepeth it with slothfulness, the same is damned.
30 Who am I that made man, saith the <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.923">Lord, that will hold him guiltless that obeys not my commandments?
31 Who am I, saith the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.798">Lord, that have promised and have not fulfilled?
32 I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.983">command and men obey not; I revoke and they receive not the blessing.
33 Then they say in their <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.945">hearts: This is not the work of the Lord, for his promises are not fulfilled. But wo unto such, for their reward lurketh beneath, and not from above.
34 And now I give unto <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.849">you further directions concerning this land.
35 It is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.938">wisdom in me that my servant Martin Harris should be an example unto the church, in laying his moneys before the bishop of the church.
36 And also, this is a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.944">law unto every man that cometh unto this land to receive an inheritance; and he shall do with his moneys according as the law directs.
37 And it is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.933">wisdom also that there should be lands purchased in Independence, for the place of the storehouse, and also for the house of the printing.
38 And other directions <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.938">concerning my servant Martin Harris shall be given him of the Spirit, that he may receive his inheritance as seemeth him good;
39 And let him <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.907">repent of his sins, for he seeketh the praise of the world.
40 And also let my <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.932">servant William W. Phelps stand in the office to which I have appointed him, and receive his inheritance in the land;
41 And also he hath need to repent, for I, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.933">Lord, am not well pleased with him, for he seeketh to excel, and he is not sufficiently meek before me.
42 <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.894">Behold, he who has repented of his sins, the same is forgiven, and I, the Lord, remember them no more.
43 By this ye may <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.915">know if a man repenteth of his sins—behold, he will confess them and forsake them.
44 And now, <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.955">verily, I say concerning the residue of the elders of my church, the time has not yet come, for many years, for them to receive their inheritance in this land, except they desire it through the prayer of faith, only as it shall be appointed unto them of the Lord.
45 For, behold, they shall <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.882">push the people together from the ends of the earth.
46 Wherefore, assemble yourselves together; and they who are not <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.952">appointed to stay in this land, let them preach the gospel in the regions round about; and after that let them return to their homes.
47 Let them <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.939">preach by the way, and bear testimony of the truth in all places, and call upon the rich, the high and the low, and the poor to repent.
48 And let them <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.904">build up churches, inasmuch as the inhabitants of the earth will repent.
49 And let there be an <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.953">agent appointed by the voice of the church, unto the church in Ohio, to receive moneys to purchase lands in Zion.
50 And I give unto my <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.950">servant Sidney Rigdon a commandment, that he shall write a description of the land of Zion, and a statement of the will of God, as it shall be made known by the Spirit unto him;
51 And an <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.964">epistle and subscription, to be presented unto all the churches to obtain moneys, to be put into the hands of the bishop, of himself or the agent, as seemeth him good or as he shall direct, to purchase lands for an inheritance for the children of God.
52 For, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.958">behold, verily I say unto you, the Lord willeth that the disciples and the children of men should open their hearts, even to purchase this whole region of country, as soon as time will permit.
53 <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.917">Behold, here is wisdom. Let them do this lest they receive none inheritance, save it be by the shedding of blood.
54 And again, <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.945">inasmuch as there is land obtained, let there be workmen sent forth of all kinds unto this land, to labor for the saints of God.
55 Let all these things be <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.941">done in order; and let the privileges of the lands be made known from time to time, by the bishop or the agent of the church.
56 And let the <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.952">work of the gathering be not in haste, nor by flight; but let it be done as it shall be counseled by the elders of the church at the conferences, according to the knowledge which they receive from time to time.
57 And let my <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.934">servant Sidney Rigdon consecrate and dedicate this land, and the spot for the temple, unto the Lord.
58 And let a conference meeting be called; and after that let my <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.970">servants Sidney Rigdon and Joseph Smith, Jun., return, and also Oliver Cowdery with them, to accomplish the residue of the work which I have appointed unto them in their own land, and the residue as shall be ruled by the conferences.
59 And let no man <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.931">return from this land except he bear record by the way, of that which he knows and most assuredly believes.
60 Let that which has been <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.969">bestowed upon Ziba Peterson be taken from him; and let him stand as a member in the church, and labor with his own hands, with the brethren, until he is sufficiently chastened for all his sins; for he confesseth them not, and he thinketh to hide them.
61 Let the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.944">residue of the elders of this church, who are coming to this land, some of whom are exceedingly blessed even above measure, also hold a conference upon this land.
62 And let my <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.904">servant Edward Partridge direct the conference which shall be held by them.
63 And let them also <span class="cw" data-tgt="phistory_of_churchhoc__vol_10.914">return, preaching the gospel by the way, bearing record of the things which are revealed unto them.
64 For, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.950">verily, the sound must go forth from this place into all the world, and unto the uttermost parts of the earth—the gospel must be preached unto every creature, with signs following them that believe.
65 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.268">behold the Son of Man cometh. Amen.
