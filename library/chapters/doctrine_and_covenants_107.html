1 There are, in the church, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">two priesthoods, namely, the Melchizedek and Aaronic, including the Levitical Priesthood.

2 Why the first is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">called the Melchizedek Priesthood is because Melchizedek was such a great high priest.
3 Before his day it was called the Holy <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Priesthood, after the Order of the Son of God.
4 But out of respect or reverence to the name of the Supreme Being, to avoid the too frequent repetition of his name, they, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">church, in ancient days, called that priesthood after Melchizedek, or the Melchizedek Priesthood.
5 All other authorities or <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">offices in the church are appendages to this priesthood.
6 But there are two divisions or grand heads—one is the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Melchizedek Priesthood, and the other is the Aaronic or Levitical Priesthood.
7 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">office of an elder comes under the priesthood of Melchizedek.
8 The Melchizedek <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Priesthood holds the right of presidency, and has power and authority over all the offices in the church in all ages of the world, to administer in spiritual things.
9 The Presidency of the High <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Priesthood, after the order of Melchizedek, have a right to officiate in all the offices in the church.
10 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.975">High priests after the order of the Melchizedek Priesthood have a right to officiate in their own standing, under the direction of the presidency, in administering spiritual things, and also in the office of an elder, priest (of the Levitical order), teacher, deacon, and member.
11 An elder has a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.930">right to officiate in his stead when the high priest is not present.
12 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.968">high priest and elder are to administer in spiritual things, agreeable to the covenants and commandments of the church; and they have a right to officiate in all these offices of the church when there are no higher authorities present.
13 The second <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.947">priesthood is called the Priesthood of Aaron, because it was conferred upon Aaron and his seed, throughout all their generations.
14 Why it is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.959">called the lesser priesthood is because it is an appendage to the greater, or the Melchizedek Priesthood, and has power in administering outward ordinances.
15 The bishopric is the presidency of this <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.889">priesthood, and holds the keys or authority of the same.
16 No man has a legal right to this office, to <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.951">hold the keys of this priesthood, except he be a literal descendant of Aaron.
17 But as a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.977">high priest of the Melchizedek Priesthood has authority to officiate in all the lesser offices, he may officiate in the office of bishop when no literal descendant of Aaron can be found, provided he is called and set apart and ordained unto this power by the hands of the Presidency of the Melchizedek Priesthood.
18 The power and <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.938">authority of the higher, or Melchizedek Priesthood, is to hold the keys of all the spiritual blessings of the church—
19 To have the privilege of receiving the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, to have the heavens <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.968">opened unto them, to commune with the general assembly and church of the Firstborn, and to enjoy the communion and presence of God the Father, and Jesus the mediator of the new covenant.
20 The power and <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.972">authority of the lesser, or Aaronic Priesthood, is to hold the keys of the ministering of angels, and to administer in outward ordinances, the letter of the gospel, the baptism of repentance for the remission of sins, agreeable to the covenants and commandments.
21 Of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.941">necessity there are presidents, or presiding officers growing out of, or appointed of or from among those who are ordained to the several offices in these two priesthoods.
22 Of the Melchizedek Priesthood, three Presiding High Priests, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pbh_robertsb_h__roberts__comprehensive_history_of_church1.000">chosen by the body, appointed and ordained to that office, and upheld by the confidence, faith, and prayer of the church, form a quorum of the Presidency of the Church.
23 The twelve traveling councilors are called to be the Twelve Apostles, or special <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.950">witnesses of the name of Christ in all the world—thus differing from other officers in the church in the duties of their calling.
24 And they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.921">form a quorum, equal in authority and power to the three presidents previously mentioned.
25 The Seventy are also called to preach the gospel, and to be especial <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.955">witnesses unto the Gentiles and in all the world—thus differing from other officers in the church in the duties of their calling.
26 And they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.933">form a quorum, equal in authority to that of the Twelve special witnesses or Apostles just named.
27 And every <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.957">decision made by either of these quorums must be by the unanimous voice of the same; that is, every member in each quorum must be agreed to its decisions, in order to make their decisions of the same power or validity one with the other—
28 A <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.905">majority may form a quorum when circumstances render it impossible to be otherwise—
29 Unless this is the case, their <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.962">decisions are not entitled to the same blessings which the decisions of a quorum of three presidents were anciently, who were ordained after the order of Melchizedek, and were righteous and holy men.
30 The decisions of these quorums, or either of them, are to be made in all righteousness, in holiness, and lowliness of heart, meekness and long-suffering, and in faith, and <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.967">virtue, and knowledge, temperance, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness and charity;
31 Because the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.916">promise is, if these things abound in them they shall not be unfruitful in the knowledge of the Lord.
32 And in case that any decision of these <span class="cw" data-tgt="pbh_robertsb_h__roberts__comprehensive_history_of_church1.000">quorums is made in unrighteousness, it may be brought before a general assembly of the several quorums, which constitute the spiritual authorities of the church; otherwise there can be no appeal from their decision.
33 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.995">Twelve are a Traveling Presiding High Council, to officiate in the name of the Lord, under the direction of the Presidency of the Church, agreeable to the institution of heaven; to build up the church, and regulate all the affairs of the same in all nations, first unto the Gentiles and secondly unto the Jews.
34 The Seventy are to act in the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.993">name of the Lord, under the direction of the Twelve or the traveling high council, in building up the church and regulating all the affairs of the same in all nations, first unto the Gentiles and then to the Jews—
35 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.989">Twelve being sent out, holding the keys, to open the door by the proclamation of the gospel of Jesus Christ, and first unto the Gentiles and then unto the Jews.
36 The standing <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.993">high councils, at the stakes of Zion, form a quorum equal in authority in the affairs of the church, in all their decisions, to the quorum of the presidency, or to the traveling high council.
37 The high council in Zion <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.950">form a quorum equal in authority in the affairs of the church, in all their decisions, to the councils of the Twelve at the stakes of Zion.
38 It is the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.950">duty of the traveling high council to call upon the Seventy, when they need assistance, to fill the several calls for preaching and administering the gospel, instead of any others.
39 It is the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.948">duty of the Twelve, in all large branches of the church, to ordain evangelical ministers, as they shall be designated unto them by revelation—
40 The order of this <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.948">priesthood was confirmed to be handed down from father to son, and rightly belongs to the literal descendants of the chosen seed, to whom the promises were made.
41 This order was <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.917">instituted in the days of Adam, and came down by lineage in the following manner:
42 From <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.994">Adam to Seth, who was ordained by Adam at the age of sixty-nine years, and was blessed by him three years previous to his (Adam's) death, and received the promise of God by his father, that his posterity should be the chosen of the Lord, and that they should be preserved unto the end of the earth;
43 Because he (Seth) was a perfect man, and his likeness was the express likeness of his father, insomuch that he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.991">seemed to be like unto his father in all things, and could be distinguished from him only by his age.
44 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.984">Enos was ordained at the age of one hundred and thirty-four years and four months, by the hand of Adam.
45 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.972">God called upon Cainan in the wilderness in the fortieth year of his age; and he met Adam in journeying to the place Shedolamak. He was eighty-seven years old when he received his ordination.
46 Mahalaleel was four hundred and ninety-six <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.985">years and seven days old when he was ordained by the hand of Adam, who also blessed him.
47 Jared was two hundred <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.976">years old when he was ordained under the hand of Adam, who also blessed him.
48 Enoch was twenty-five <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.906">years old when he was ordained under the hand of Adam; and he was sixty-five and Adam blessed him.
49 And he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.990">saw the Lord, and he walked with him, and was before his face continually; and he walked with God three hundred and sixty-five years, making him four hundred and thirty years old when he was translated.
50 Methuselah was one hundred <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.975">years old when he was ordained under the hand of Adam.
51 Lamech was thirty-two <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.977">years old when he was ordained under the hand of Seth.
52 Noah was ten <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.885">years old when he was ordained under the hand of Methuselah.
53 Three <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.975">years previous to the death of Adam, he called Seth, Enos, Cainan, Mahalaleel, Jared, Enoch, and Methuselah, who were all high priests, with the residue of his posterity who were righteous, into the valley of Adam-ondi-Ahman, and there bestowed upon them his last blessing.
54 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.685">Lord appeared unto them, and they rose up and blessed Adam, and called him Michael, the prince, the archangel.
55 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.960">Lord administered comfort unto Adam, and said unto him: I have set thee to be at the head; a multitude of nations shall come of thee, and thou art a prince over them forever.
56 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.990">Adam stood up in the midst of the congregation; and, notwithstanding he was bowed down with age, being full of the Holy Ghost, predicted whatsoever should befall his posterity unto the latest generation.
57 These things were all <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.863">written in the book of Enoch, and are to be testified of in due time.
58 It is the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.761">duty of the Twelve, also, to ordain and set in order all the other officers of the church, agreeable to the revelation which says:
59 To the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.926">church of Christ in the land of Zion, in addition to the church laws respecting church business—
60 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.929">Verily, I say unto you, saith the Lord of Hosts, there must needs be presiding elders to preside over those who are of the office of an elder;
61 And also priests to <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.840">preside over those who are of the office of a priest;
62 And also teachers to <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.905">preside over those who are of the office of a teacher, in like manner, and also the deacons—
63 Wherefore, from deacon to teacher, and from teacher to priest, and from priest to elder, severally as they are appointed, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.953">according to the covenants and commandments of the church.
64 Then <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.816">comes the High Priesthood, which is the greatest of all.
65 Wherefore, it must needs be that one be appointed of the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.939">High Priesthood to preside over the priesthood, and he shall be called President of the High Priesthood of the Church;
66 Or, in other words, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.872">Presiding High Priest over the High Priesthood of the Church.
67 From the same <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.890">comes the administering of ordinances and blessings upon the church, by the laying on of the hands.
68 Wherefore, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.939">office of a bishop is not equal unto it; for the office of a bishop is in administering all temporal things;
69 Nevertheless a bishop must be chosen from the High <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.914">Priesthood, unless he is a literal descendant of Aaron;
70 For unless he is a literal <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.895">descendant of Aaron he cannot hold the keys of that priesthood.
71 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.938">Nevertheless, a high priest, that is, after the order of Melchizedek, may be set apart unto the ministering of temporal things, having a knowledge of them by the Spirit of truth;
72 And also to be a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.962">judge in Israel, to do the business of the church, to sit in judgment upon transgressors upon testimony as it shall be laid before him according to the laws, by the assistance of his counselors, whom he has chosen or will choose among the elders of the church.
73 This is the duty of a bishop who is not a literal <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.919">descendant of Aaron, but has been ordained to the High Priesthood after the order of Melchizedek.
74 Thus shall he be a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.970">judge, even a common judge among the inhabitants of Zion, or in a stake of Zion, or in any branch of the church where he shall be set apart unto this ministry, until the borders of Zion are enlarged and it becomes necessary to have other bishops or judges in Zion or elsewhere.
75 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.880">inasmuch as there are other bishops appointed they shall act in the same office.
76 But a literal <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.970">descendant of Aaron has a legal right to the presidency of this priesthood, to the keys of this ministry, to act in the office of bishop independently, without counselors, except in a case where a President of the High Priesthood, after the order of Melchizedek, is tried, to sit as a judge in Israel.
77 And the decision of either of these <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.885">councils, agreeable to the commandment which says:
78 Again, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.968">verily, I say unto you, the most important business of the church, and the most difficult cases of the church, inasmuch as there is not satisfaction upon the decision of the bishop or judges, it shall be handed over and carried up unto the council of the church, before the Presidency of the High Priesthood.
79 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.962">Presidency of the council of the High Priesthood shall have power to call other high priests, even twelve, to assist as counselors; and thus the Presidency of the High Priesthood and its counselors shall have power to decide upon testimony according to the laws of the church.
80 And after this decision it shall be had in <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.948">remembrance no more before the Lord; for this is the highest council of the church of God, and a final decision upon controversies in spiritual matters.
81 There is not any <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.892">person belonging to the church who is exempt from this council of the church.
82 And inasmuch as a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.954">President of the High Priesthood shall transgress, he shall be had in remembrance before the common council of the church, who shall be assisted by twelve counselors of the High Priesthood;
83 And their decision upon his <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.878">head shall be an end of controversy concerning him.
84 Thus, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.940">none shall be exempted from the justice and the laws of God, that all things may be done in order and in solemnity before him, according to truth and righteousness.
85 And again, verily I say unto you, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.989">duty of a president over the office of a deacon is to preside over twelve deacons, to sit in council with them, and to teach them their duty, edifying one another, as it is given according to the covenants.
86 And also the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.986">duty of the president over the office of the teachers is to preside over twenty-four of the teachers, and to sit in council with them, teaching them the duties of their office, as given in the covenants.
87 Also the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.985">duty of the president over the Priesthood of Aaron is to preside over forty-eight priests, and sit in council with them, to teach them the duties of their office, as is given in the covenants—
88 This <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.829">president is to be a bishop; for this is one of the duties of this priesthood.
89 Again, the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.985">duty of the president over the office of elders is to preside over ninety-six elders, and to sit in council with them, and to teach them according to the covenants.
90 This <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.973">presidency is a distinct one from that of the seventy, and is designed for those who do not travel into all the world.
91 And again, the duty of the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.972">President of the office of the High Priesthood is to preside over the whole church, and to be like unto Moses—
92 Behold, here is wisdom; yea, to be a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.984">seer, a revelator, a translator, and a prophet, having all the gifts of God which he bestows upon the head of the church.
93 And it is according to the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.942">vision showing the order of the Seventy, that they should have seven presidents to preside over them, chosen out of the number of the seventy;
94 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.955">seventh president of these presidents is to preside over the six;
95 And these <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.919">seven presidents are to choose other seventy besides the first seventy to whom they belong, and are to preside over them;
96 And also other <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.975">seventy, until seven times seventy, if the labor in the vineyard of necessity requires it.
97 And these <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.971">seventy are to be traveling ministers, unto the Gentiles first and also unto the Jews.
98 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.990">Whereas other officers of the church, who belong not unto the Twelve, neither to the Seventy, are not under the responsibility to travel among all nations, but are to travel as their circumstances shall allow, notwithstanding they may hold as high and responsible offices in the church.
99 Wherefore, now let every man <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.905">learn his duty, and to act in the office in which he is appointed, in all diligence.
100 He that is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.959">slothful shall not be counted worthy to stand, and he that learns not his duty and shows himself not approved shall not be counted worthy to stand. Even so. Amen.
