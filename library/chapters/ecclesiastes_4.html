4

1 SO I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.748">returned, and considered all the oppressions that are done under the sun: and behold the tears of [such as were] oppressed, and they had no comforter; and on the side of their oppressors [there was] power; but they had no comforter.
2 Wherefore I <span class="cw" data-tgt="psefariakabbalah__zohar__vayeshev_16_1780.675">praised the dead which are already dead more than the living which are yet alive.
3 Yea, better [is he] than both they, which hath not yet been, who hath not <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">seen the evil work that is done under the sun.
4 Again, I considered all travail, and every right <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.990">work, that for this a man is envied of his neighbour. This [is] also vanity and vexation of spirit.
5 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pchurch_fatherschurch_fathers__anf_vol20.297">fool foldeth his hands together, and eateth his own flesh.
6 Better [is] an <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">handful [with] quietness, than both the hands full [with] travail and vexation of spirit.
7 Then I returned, and I saw vanity under the sun.
8 There is <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">one [alone], and [there is] not a second; yea, he hath neither child nor brother: yet [is there] no end of all his labour; neither is his eye satisfied with riches; neither [saith he], For whom do I labour, and bereave my soul of good? This [is] also vanity, yea, it [is] a sore travail.
9 Two [are] better than one; because they have a good reward for their labour.
10 For if they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.779">fall, the one will lift up his fellow: but woe to him [that is] alone when he falleth; for [he hath] not another to help him up.
11 Again, if <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.856">two lie together, then they have heat: but how can one be warm [alone]?
12 And if one prevail against him, two shall withstand him; and a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.993">threefold cord is not quickly broken.
13 Better [is] a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.851">poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be admonished.
14 For <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.929">out of prison he cometh to reign; whereas also [he that is] born in his kingdom becometh poor.
15 I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.852">considered all the living which walk under the sun, with the second child that shall stand up in his stead.
16 [There is] no <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.935">end of all the people, [even] of all that have been before them: they also that come after shall not rejoice in him. Surely this also [is] vanity and vexation of spirit.
