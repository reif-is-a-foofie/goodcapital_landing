Esther

7

1 SO the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.582">king and Haman came to banquet with Esther the queen.
2 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.890">king said again unto Esther on the second day at the banquet of wine, What [is] thy petition, queen Esther? and it shall be granted thee: and what [is] thy request? and it shall be performed, [even] to the half of the kingdom.
3 Then Esther the queen answered and said, If I have found favour in thy <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.730">sight, O king, and if it please the king, let my life be given me at my petition, and my people at my request:
4 For we are <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.663">sold, I and my people, to be destroyed, to be slain, and to perish. But if we had been sold for bondmen and bondwomen, I had held my tongue, although the enemy could not countervail the king's damage.
5 Then the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.460">king Ahasuerus answered and said unto Esther the queen, Who is he, and where is he, that durst presume in his heart to do so?
6 And Esther said, The adversary and <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.632">enemy [is] this wicked Haman. Then Haman was afraid before the king and the queen.
7 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.953">king arising from the banquet of wine in his wrath [went] into the palace garden: and Haman stood up to make request for his life to Esther the queen; for he saw that there was evil determined against him by the king.
8 Then the king returned out of the palace garden into the place of the banquet of wine; and <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.956">Haman was fallen upon the bed whereon Esther [was]. Then said the king, Will he force the queen also before me in the house? As the word went out of the king's mouth, they covered Haman's face.
9 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.889">Harbonah, one of the chamberlains, said before the king, Behold also, the gallows fifty cubits high, which Haman had made for Mordecai, who had spoken good for the king, standeth in the house of Haman. Then the king said, Hang him thereon.
10 So they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.896">hanged Haman on the gallows that he had prepared for Mordecai. Then was the king's wrath pacified.
