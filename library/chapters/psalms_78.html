Donaldson Commentary

He was to be born of a virgin; he was to be named Immanuel, which means “God with us.” (See Isa. 7:14 [Isaiah 7:14] and Matt. 1:23 [Matthew 1:23].) He was to come out of Egypt (see Hosea 11:1 and Matt. 2:13–23 [Matthew 2:13–23]), and yet be born in Bethlehem of Judea. (See Micah 5:2.) His enemies would cause great mourning for children in Bethlehem. (See Jer. 31:15 [Jeremiah 31:15] and Matt. 2:18 [Matthew 2:18].) And he would finally be called a Nazarene; the Nazarenes were despised, and so people could call him a Nazarene, meaning “despised.” (See Isa. 53:3 [Isaiah 53:3] and Matt. 2:23 [Matthew 2:23].) He was to do many marvelous acts which their recipients were not to make known in the streets. (See Isa. 42:2 [Isaiah 42:2] and Matt. 12:19 [Matthew 12:19].) He would cast out devils and heal the sick. (See Isa. 53:4 [Isaiah 53:4] and Matt. 8:17 [Matthew 8:17].) He was to teach in parables. (See Ps. 78:2 [Psalm 78:2] and Matt. 13:35 [Matthew 13:35].) When he was proclaimed king, he would come lowly, and riding upon an ass, and upon a colt, the foal of an ass. (See Zech. 9:9 [Zechariah 9:9] and Matt. 21:4–9 [Matthew 21:4–9].) (S.
