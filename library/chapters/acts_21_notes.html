The Long Journey of Paul Lesson 38: Acts 21-28 Lee Donaldson, Editorial Consultant _ New Testament for Latter-day Saint Families_ Twice in the book of Acts the apostle Paul claims his rights or privileges as a Roman citizen. On both occasions his preaching ignited strong reactions against him and required the authorities to become involved. First, early in his missionary journeys in Philippi (which happened to be a Roman colony) he told the authorities that he was a Roman citizen (Acts 16:35-38) and again many years later during his final visit in Jerusalem (Acts 22:24-29).
