"Salutations in the Marketplaces . . . " (Mark 12:38.) In the New Testament, the words marketplace and market are the translation of the Greek word agora. These English words do not fully convey the function of the agora as a public gathering center for social life as well as a place of commerce or a marketplace. The agora was usually a large open space near the city center that had three basic functions. It was a commercial center, containing shops, stalls, tradesmen, and merchants. Public buildings such as the city hall, courts, and temples typically surrounded the agora, making it also the municipal center of the city. The third function of the agora was as a place of socializing and recreation.
