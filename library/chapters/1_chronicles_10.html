1 NOW the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.966">Philistines fought against Israel; and the men of Israel fled from before the Philistines, and fell down slain in mount Gilboa.

3 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">battle went sore against Saul, and the archers hit him, and he was wounded of the archers.
4 Then said <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.864">Saul to his armourbearer, Draw thy sword, and thrust me through therewith; lest these uncircumcised come and abuse me. But his armourbearer would not; for he was sore afraid. So Saul took a sword, and fell upon it.
5 And when his armourbearer <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.756">saw that Saul was dead, he fell likewise on the sword, and died.
6 So <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.476">Saul died, and his three sons, and all his house died together.
7 And when all the men of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.890">Israel that [were] in the valley saw that they fled, and that Saul and his sons were dead, then they forsook their cities, and fled: and the Philistines came and dwelt in them.
8 And it came to <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">pass on the morrow, when the Philistines came to strip the slain, that they found Saul and his sons fallen in mount Gilboa.
9 And when they had <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.910">stripped him, they took his head, and his armour, and sent into the land of the Philistines round about, to carry tidings unto their idols, and to the people.
10 And they put his <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.934">armour in the house of their gods, and fastened his head in the temple of Dagon.
11 And when all Jabeshgilead <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.592">heard all that the Philistines had done to Saul,
12 They <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.833">arose, all the valiant men, and took away the body of Saul, and the bodies of his sons, and brought them to Jabesh, and buried their bones under the oak in Jabesh, and fasted seven days.
13 So <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.772">Saul died for his transgression which he committed against the LORD, [even] against the word of the LORD, which he kept not, and also for asking [counsel] of [one that had] a familiar spirit, to enquire [of it];
14 And enquired not of the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.808">LORD: therefore he slew him, and turned the kingdom unto David the son of Jesse.
