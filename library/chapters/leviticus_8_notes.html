The public consecration of the Aaronic priests involved washing, anointing, and clothing them in priestly garments; afterward, sacrifices and a ceremonial meal were conducted. Note the use of the Urim and Thummim (Lev. 8:8 and fn.). The participants stayed at the tabernacle for a week (Lev. 8:33-35). (Ellis T. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament) The ordination of Aaron and his sons to the priesthood is described in Leviticus 8-9. The ordination is, in a very real sense, like a coronation, complete with anointing, investiture, sacrifices, exhortations, and, in the case of Aaron himself, crowning. Moses gathered the congregation to the door of the tabernacle (Leviticus 8:3-4), where he washed Aaron and his sons (8:5-6). Aaron was then dressed like a priestly king, including a miter or crown (Leviticus 8:7-9). The tabernacle, its altar and vessels, were then anointed (Leviticus 8:10-11), as was Aaron (Leviticus 8:12). (John A. Tvedtnes in, By Study and Also by Faith 2: 216.)
