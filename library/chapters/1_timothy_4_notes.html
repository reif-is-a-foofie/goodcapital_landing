The word order as arranged in the King James Version is an unfortunate one, for it conveys something different from what was intended. Literally, the Greek reads, "Bodily exercise for a little is profitable, but godliness for all things profitable is." The meaning is not that physical exercise is worthless, but, rather, that its usefulness is limited; it is profitable only in a few ways. But godliness (or spiritual exercise) has unlimited usefulness; it is profitable in every way. Physical exercise profits the body, which shall perish, but godliness profits both the body and the spirit. (Life and Teachings of Jesus and His Apostles, p. 363)
