In this psalm of supplication, after the salutation, the worshipper expresses confidence in his contact with the Lord and summarizes truths about Him for the benefit of fellow worshippers. (On "leasing," Ps. 5:6, see commentary on Ps. 4:2.) (Ellis T. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament, p.415)

McConkie / Teachings of Joseph Smith
As commonly used today, vanity means shallow pride. Biblical passages relative to vanity, however, have been translated from original words having the meanings of emptiness, fruitlessness, and worthlessness. The Hebrew word translated vanity in David's statement, "How long will ye love vanity" (Ps. 4:2), for instance, is one which indicates a sense of empty failure. This same Biblical meaning of vanity seems to have been carried over into the modem revelations. (D. & C. 20:5; 84:54-55.)McConkie, Mormon Doctrine

That which is of God is light; and he that receiveth light and continueth in God, receiveth more light; and that light groweth brighter and brighter until the perfect day." (D. & C. 50:23-24.) And if your eye be single to my glory, your whole bodies shall be filled with light, and there shall be no darkness in you; and that body which is filled with light comprehendeth all things."
