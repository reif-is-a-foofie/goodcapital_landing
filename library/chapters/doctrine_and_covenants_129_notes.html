Donaldson Commentary●●●○○Cross-ref · Moroni 10:34●●●○○
"In the same sense that flesh and blood is used to signify mortality, flesh and bones is the scriptural phrase meaning immortality, the state after the resurrection when all men will have spiritual bodies. Thus, appearing to his disciples after his resurrection, our Lord said: 'Handle me, and see; for a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have.' (Luke 24:39.) In exactly the same sense, 'The Father has a body of flesh and bones as tangible as man's' (D. & C. 130:22), for he too is a resurrected, immortal Being." (Bruce R. Mc Conkie, Mormon Doctrine, 1966, pp. 288- 289.)
