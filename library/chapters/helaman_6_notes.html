And thus we see A phrase often used by Book of Mormon record keepers to emphasize a principle, precept, or consequence that can be learned from the event(s) or behavior(s) just discussed. It signals the reader that something important will immediately follow. Simply put, by using the phrase "and thus we see," the writer is saying, "If you missed the point of what you just read, I will now explain the message you should have received." Because Book of Mormon record keepers could not include even "a hundredth part" of their history, the use of this literary device served as a significant tool helping them to summarize, conclude, encapsulate, and highlight important concepts (Jacob 3:13; W of M 1:5; 3 Ne. 5:8; Ether 15:33).
