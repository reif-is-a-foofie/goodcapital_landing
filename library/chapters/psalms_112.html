Donaldson●●○○○

This psalm, with the five immediately following, were called the great Hallelujah, and used to be sung at the Passover supper (Matt. 26:30).

The Hallel. (Ps. 113-18.) Hallel, which means "Praise [God]!" is the series of psalms of praise that were recited at all new moons and festivals and that also had an important place in the Passover ritual. The hymn that Jesus and the Apostles sang at the Last Supper may have been one or more of these psalms. (See Matthew 26:30.) Many concepts and phrases from Psalm 118 are woven throughout the Lord's teachings. (See, for example, Psalms 118:17 and John 11:26; Psalms 118:22-23 and Mark 12:10-11; Psalms 118:26 and Matthew 23:39.) (Stephen and Shirley Smith Ricks, "Jewish Religious Education in the Meridian of Time," Ensign, Oct. 1987, 62)
