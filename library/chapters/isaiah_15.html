1THE burden of Moab. Because in the night Ar of Moab is laid waste, [and] brought to silence; because in the night Kir of Moab is laid waste, [and] brought to silence;
Donaldson Commentary

Moab, which was named after the firstborn son of Lot's eldest daughter (Gen. 19:37), was the nation that bordered the Dead Sea on the east. The prophet warns the Moabites that they will be destroyed, despite the fact that they are prosperous in trading and commerce. The listing of sixteen cities and places in this section, which are located in both the northern and southern regions of Moab, indicates that all of Moab will be affected by the devastation. (Parry, Understanding Isaiah, 156)

Donaldson Commentary

Bajith (Heb. Bayith) "house"

Medeba - "water of rest"

The inhabitants of Moab go to their temples (Hebrew Byth, "temple") and high places to mourn and lament because of the destruction. It was customary for males to demonstrate their sorrow by shaving their heads and beards (22:12; Micah 1:16). The once proud inhabitants of Moab are now reduced to humility and shame.
