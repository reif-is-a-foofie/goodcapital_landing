Exodus

36

1 THEN wrought Bezaleel and Aholiab, and every wise hearted man, in whom the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.738">LORD put wisdom and understanding to know how to work all manner of work for the service of the sanctuary, according to all that the LORD had commanded.
2 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">Moses called Bezaleel and Aholiab, and every wise hearted man, in whose heart the LORD had put wisdom, [even] every one whose heart stirred him up to come unto the work to do it:
3 And they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">received of Moses all the offering, which the children of Israel had brought for the work of the service of the sanctuary, to make it [withal]. And they brought yet unto him free offerings every morning.
4 And all the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.936">wise men, that wrought all the work of the sanctuary, came every man from his work which they made;
5 And they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">spake unto Moses, saying, The people bring much more than enough for the service of the work, which the LORD commanded to make.
6 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">Moses gave commandment, and they caused it to be proclaimed throughout the camp, saying, Let neither man nor woman make any more work for the offering of the sanctuary. So the people were restrained from bringing.
7 For the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">stuff they had was sufficient for all the work to make it, and too much.
8 And every wise hearted man among them that wrought the work of the tabernacle made ten curtains [of] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.845">fine twined linen, and blue, and purple, and scarlet: [with] cherubims of cunning work made he them.
9 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">length of one curtain [was] twenty and eight cubits, and the breadth of one curtain four cubits: the curtains [were] all of one size.
10 And he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.972">coupled the five curtains one unto another: and [the other] five curtains he coupled one unto another.
11 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.996">loops of blue on the edge of one curtain from the selvedge in the coupling: likewise he made in the uttermost side of [another] curtain, in the coupling of the second.
12 Fifty <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.996">loops made he in one curtain, and fifty loops made he in the edge of the curtain which [was] in the coupling of the second: the loops held one [curtain] to another.
13 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.944">fifty taches of gold, and coupled the curtains one unto another with the taches: so it became one tabernacle.
14 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.991">curtains [of] goats' [hair] for the tent over the tabernacle: eleven curtains he made them.
15 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.863">length of one curtain [was] thirty cubits, and four cubits [was] the breadth of one curtain: the eleven curtains [were] of one size.
16 And he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.580">coupled five curtains by themselves, and six curtains by themselves.
17 And he made fifty <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.996">loops upon the uttermost edge of the curtain in the coupling, and fifty loops made he upon the edge of the curtain which coupleth the second.
18 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.895">fifty taches [of] brass to couple the tent together, that it might be one.
19 And he made a <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.994">covering for the tent [of] rams' skins dyed red, and a covering [of] badgers' skins above [that].
20 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.444">boards for the tabernacle [of] shittim wood, standing up.
21 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">length of a board [was] ten cubits, and the breadth of a board one cubit and a half.
22 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.992">One board had two tenons, equally distant one from another: thus did he make for all the boards of the tabernacle.
23 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.958">boards for the tabernacle; twenty boards for the south side southward:
24 And forty <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.995">sockets of silver he made under the twenty boards; two sockets under one board for his two tenons, and two sockets under another board for his two tenons.
25 And for the other <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.983">side of the tabernacle, [which is] toward the north corner, he made twenty boards,
26 And their forty <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.992">sockets of silver; two sockets under one board, and two sockets under another board.
27 And for the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.303">sides of the tabernacle westward he made six boards.
28 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.982">two boards made he for the corners of the tabernacle in the two sides.
29 And they were <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.992">coupled beneath, and coupled together at the head thereof, to one ring: thus he did to both of them in both the corners.
30 And there were eight boards; and their <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.700">sockets [were] sixteen sockets of silver, under every board two sockets.
31 And he made <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.821">bars of shittim wood; five for the boards of the one side of the tabernacle,
32 And five <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.992">bars for the boards of the other side of the tabernacle, and five bars for the boards of the tabernacle for the sides westward.
33 And he made the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.970">middle bar to shoot through the boards from the one end to the other.
34 And he overlaid the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.993">boards with gold, and made their rings [of] gold [to be] places for the bars, and overlaid the bars with gold.
35 And he made a vail [of] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.632">blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen: [with] cherubims made he it of cunning work.
36 And he made thereunto four pillars [of] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.944">shittim [wood], and overlaid them with gold: their hooks [were of] gold; and he cast for them four sockets of silver.
37 And he made an hanging for the tabernacle door [of] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.991">blue, and purple, and scarlet, and fine twined linen, of needlework;
38 And the five <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.991">pillars of it with their hooks: and he overlaid their chapiters and their fillets with gold: but their five sockets [were of] brass.
