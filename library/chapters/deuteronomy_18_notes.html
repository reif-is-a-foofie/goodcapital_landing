Donaldson●●●●○

The Canaanites were a superstitious people who believed in and practiced divination and black magic. An enchanter inspects the entrails of dead animals, watches the flight of birds, or used other means to predict the future. A charmer employs spells and incantations in predicting future events. Consulters with familiar spirits try to contact the spirit of a departed person to learn things not known to human beings. A wizard is a male witch. A necromancer, like one who consults with familiar spirits, seeks the secrets of the spirit world by inquiring of the dead. All of these activities were forbidden to ancient Israel. They were admonished to heed the words of their living prophet. (CES Old Testament Student Manual: Genesis-2 Samuel, 226)

Donaldson Commentary●●●○○Cross-ref · Mosiah 20:1●●●○○
1-26. The nation of Israel was formed by God with a prophet, chosen by him, to stand at its head. The law of Moses, which governed the nation, was given by revelation and could only be amended in the same manner. It was the divine intent that Israel always be led by prophets. This being the case, it was essential that citizenry of the nation be able to discern between the true and the false prophet. To that end Moses, the first prophet to the nation of Israel, established the first scriptural test by which prophets were to be discerned.
