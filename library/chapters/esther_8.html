Esther

8

1 ON that day did the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_esther_8_10.499">king Ahasuerus give the house of Haman the Jews' enemy unto Esther the queen. And Mordecai came before the king; for Esther had told what he [was] unto her.
2 And the king took off his ring, which he had taken from <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.921">Haman, and gave it unto Mordecai. And Esther set Mordecai over the house of Haman.
3 And Esther <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.957">spake yet again before the king, and fell down at his feet, and besought him with tears to put away the mischief of Haman the Agagite, and his device that he had devised against the Jews.
4 Then the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">king held out the golden sceptre toward Esther. So Esther arose, and stood before the king,
5 And said, If it please the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.991">king, and if I have found favour in his sight, and the thing [seem] right before the king, and I [be] pleasing in his eyes, let it be written to reverse the letters devised by Haman the son of Hammedatha the Agagite, which he wrote to destroy the Jews which [are] in all the king's provinces:
6 For how can I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">endure to see the evil that shall come unto my people? or how can I endure to see the destruction of my kindred?
7 Then the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.710">king Ahasuerus said unto Esther the queen and to Mordecai the Jew, Behold, I have given Esther the house of Haman, and him they have hanged upon the gallows, because he laid his hand upon the Jews.
8 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">Write ye also for the Jews, as it liketh you, in the king's name, and seal [it] with the king's ring: for the writing which is written in the king's name, and sealed with the king's ring, may no man reverse.
9 Then were the king's scribes called at that time in the third month, that [is], the month Sivan, on the three and twentieth [day] thereof; and it was written <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.984">according to all that Mordecai commanded unto the Jews, and to the lieutenants, and the deputies and rulers of the provinces which [are] from India unto Ethiopia, an hundred twenty and seven provinces, unto every province according to the writing thereof, and unto every people after their language, and to the Jews according to their writing, and according to their language.
10 And he wrote in the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.257">king Ahasuerus' name, and sealed [it] with the king's ring, and sent letters by posts on horseback, [and] riders on mules, camels, [and] young dromedaries:
11 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.503">Wherein the king granted the Jews which [were] in every city to gather themselves together, and to stand for their life, to destroy, to slay, and to cause to perish, all the power of the people and province that would assault them, [both] little ones and women, and [to take] the spoil of them for a prey,
12 Upon one day in all the provinces of king Ahasuerus, [namely], upon the <span class="cw" data-tgt="papocryphalxx_apocrypha__charles_apot_vol1_apocrypha0.475">thirteenth [day] of the twelfth month, which [is] the month Adar.
13 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.736">copy of the writing for a commandment to be given in every province [was] published unto all people, and that the Jews should be ready against that day to avenge themselves on their enemies.
14 [So] the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.476">posts that rode upon mules [and] camels went out, being hastened and pressed on by the king's commandment. And the decree was given at Shushan the palace.
15 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.480">Mordecai went out from the presence of the king in royal apparel of blue and white, and with a great crown of gold, and with a garment of fine linen and purple: and the city of Shushan rejoiced and was glad.
16 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="psefariatalmud__megillah_16b_80.301">Jews had light, and gladness, and joy, and honour.
17 And in every <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.346">province, and in every city, whithersoever the king's commandment and his decree came, the Jews had joy and gladness, a feast and a good day. And many of the people of the land became Jews; for the fear of the Jews fell upon them.
