John introduces this section, according to the KJV, with the words: "And there appeared a great wonder in heaven." Here "wonder" would be better translated as "sign" (Greek smeion). A wonder is a surprise of great magnitude, but a sign is replete with meaning and points to a definite subject or object. The word denotes an optical impression through which one gains confirmations. [TDNT, s.v., smeion. See Moulton, Vocabulary, s.v. smeion where the word is used for a "sign," "seal," or "distinguishing mark" that is visible to the eye.] (Richard D. Draper, Opening the Seven Seals: The Visions of John the Revelator [Salt Lake City: Deseret Book Co., 1991], 129.)

Celestial glory surrounds her as a garment, showing that the power of God is upon her. The Greek word used here, peribebmen, "having been clothed," suggests that thewoman wears the glory as a garment but that it does not belong to her. It is reflected by her. Thus, she manifests the light of Christ but is not the source of that light. There is also a secondary meaning of the Greek verb periball, translated "clothed," that isvery likely at play here. The word means "to throw up a rampart around" (cf. Luke 19:43). John's imagery could, therefore, represent the glory of God acting as a bulwark to protect the Church. (Richard D. Draper, Opening the Seven Seals: The Visions of John the Revelator [Salt Lake City: Deseret Book Co., 1991], 129.)

a woman clothed with the sun. Joseph Smith's translation of 12:7 makes clear that the woman represents the true Church of God, which is not just an organization but many righteous individuals joined together by covenant. In being clothed with the sun, the woman represents the destiny of those who join themselves to her and follow the Lord with pure hearts: they will be privileged to enter the celestial kingdom, "whose glory is [like] that of the sun" (D&C 76:70). In being clothed with the sun, the true Church becomes like unto God himself, who is clothed "with light as with a garment" (Ps. 104:2). (Donald W. Parry and Jay A. Parry, Understanding the Book of Revelation [Salt Lake City: Deseret Book Co., 1998], 148.)

the moon under her feet. The moon represents a lesser glory than that of the sun (1 Cor. 15:40-41). The glory of the moon, which is terrestrial, is lesser than, or inferior to, the glory enjoyed by the Church and its faithful adherents. This expression could also mean that the woman has power or dominance over the moon; in other words, it may suggest that those of the Church who attain a celestial glory will have stewardship and ascendancy over those who attain a lesser glory. (Donald W. Parry and Jay A. Parry, Understanding the Book of Revelation [Salt Lake City: Deseret Book Co., 1998], 150.)

a crown of twelve stars. The head of the Church is the Twelve Apostles of the Lord, who direct the Church in righteousness, under Christ (1 Ne. 1:10). This system of Church government continues to our day-the presiding officer of the Church is the senior apostle. The crown signifies the preeminent position of the apostles; stars often symbolize individual human beings (Gen. 37:9; Job 38:7; see commentary on 1:16, 20). The crown of twelve stars may also represent the twelve tribes of Israel, through whom we are able to partake of the Abrahamic covenant. (Donald W. Parry and Jay A. Parry, Understanding the Book of Revelation [Salt Lake City: Deseret Book Co., 1998], 151.)

You may recall from verse five that the JST changes "days" to be "years." 1260 days was often used as the same as 42 months or 3 and one-half years. The same number may be suggested by "time, and times, and half a time." This can be written 1 + 2 + 1/2 or "3 1/2." Again, we must note that the Hebrew mind usually did not use numbers in strictly a quantitative sense. They were often used symbolically. That is likely the case with these numbers. According to J. M. Ford, "…Three and one-half, half of seven (Rev. 11: 9, 11), denotes what is arrested midway in its normal course. This apocalyptic usage of this number goes back to Dan 7: 25; cf. 9: 27, 12: 7; bear in mind that the period from the persecution by Antiochus Epiphanes to the rededication of the Temple was about three and one-half years, i.e. from 168/7 to 165/4. The number six also connotes deficit, a failure to attain the completeness of seven, while eight designates superabundance. Forty-two has two-fold symbolism. It is pejorative, since it is the result of six multiplied by seven, i.e. 'perfection missing the mark.' It is also messianic, since it is the result of three multiplied by fourteen. The number fourteen is the sum of the

letters which in Hebrew stand for four, six, four. Daledh is the fourth letter of the hebrew alphabet, and waw is the sixth; thus four-six four stands for d-w-d, i.e. David. Matt 1: 1-17 divides the genealogy of Christ from Abraham into three groups of fourteen names each. Christ is the new David." (J. M. Ford, Revelation, The Anchor Bible Series, Vol. 38, p. 170.)

Barclay is in agreement on this point: "Its meaning is three and a half years. That is what forty-two months, and twelve hundred and sixty days -- by Jewish figuring -- are. A time, times and half a time is equal to one year plus two years plus half a year. "The origin of the phrase comes from that most terrible time in Jewish history when Antiochus Epiphanes, King of Syria, tried to force Greek language, culture and worship upon the Jews and was met with the most violent and stubborn resistance. The roll of the martyrs was immense but the dreadful process was finally halted by the rising of Judas Maccabaeus. "Judas and his heroic followers waged guerrilla warfare and won the most amazing victories. Finally Antiochus and his forces were driven out and the Temple was restored and cleansed. The point is that this dreadful period lasted from June 168 B.C. to December 165 B.C. (To this day the Jews celebrate in December the Festival of Hanukah which commemorates the restoration and the cleansing of the Temple.) That is to say this dreadful time lasted almost exactly three and a half years. It was during that time that Daniel was written and the phrase was coined which ever afterwards was stamped on the Jewish mind as indicating a period of terror and suffering and martydom." (William Barclay, The Revelation of John, Vol. 2, Revised Edition, pp. 168-69.)

It seems quite clear from the above references that these numbers may symbolize 'apostasy.' This may be the meaning that Joseph Smith was attempting to convey by changing 'days' to 'years.' Verse 14 could be: Time = 360; times = 720; half-a-time = 180; total = 1260 years. A few have taken the meaning to be much more literal. One very literal interpretation is given in the recent U. S. Midwest CES Symposium paper by Kent Dunford, "Unraveling the Book of Revelation." This view, reported by Brother Dunford, attempts to figure the date of the Apostasy by counting back 1260 years from the Restoration. Using our own calendar, of course, would give us the date of 570 A.D. Such a computation would not be quite as easy as expected, however. As Dunford's account explains, the Hebrews were functioning under the old lunar calendar which only had 354 days per year. (Harper's Bible Dictionary, p. 760) The 1260 of their lunar years would be the equivalent of 1223 of our current calendar years. This would give us the date of 607 A.D. if we subtracted back from the Restoration. Interestingly, this is the year that the Roman Bishop was recognized throughout as the leader of the Church. A couple of sources, cited by Kent, offer insight into this event: "Boniface III, who was pope for only ten months in the year 607, was the first to whom the title of Universal Bishop of Christendom was conceded by the Greek Emperor Phocas. By this imperial decree the papacy was finally recognized as an established institution." (The New International Encyclopedia, Vol. III, p. 278.) "At last out comes an edict from Phocas (607) commanding that the church of Rome should be styled and esteemed 'the head of all churches,' and the Pope 'universal bishop.' A rare charter, sure, not founded upon the canons of the church, but upon an imperial edict, and this the Church of Rome sat as queen." (William Cave, D.D., Primitive Christianity, Oxford, 1844, p. 462.) It is important to note that this approach seeking a specific date is purely speculative. There are not many, even among the most extreme literalists, who believe that the apostasy began in a specific year. Nor is it logical to believe that it was brought on by a particular event, such as the recognition of the Bishop of Rome as Pope. The apostasy began even during the life of Paul and Peter. It was a long, drawn out, development. The number 1260, like the number 3 1/2 or 42 can symbolize apostasy, without determining a specific date. We must remind ourselves of the symbolic nature of numbers in the Hebrew world. It might have been enough for them to realize the meaning without pinpointing the date.

The additional revelation given concerning The Great and Abominable Church in 1 Nephi 13 and 14 seems to have more parallel with Revelation 17 than this chapter. The correlations will be discussed in more detail under the notes in that chapter. This chapter is a good place to keep in mind the counsel that the Prophet Joseph Smith gave the early missionaries: "Declare the first principles, and let mysteries alone, lest ye be overthrown. Never meddle with the visions of beasts and subjects you do not understand." After reading Revelation 13: 1-8 as an example of a non-proselyting passage, he mentioned several of the common interpretations of the day, and then he declared them all as false. He gave some general explanations which will be quoted throughout this outline, but he refrained from identifying specific nations and kingdoms who fulfill these prophecies. It might be well to follow the Prophet's example. So, while we will look at possibilities, it is important to remind ourselves of the tentativeness of our suppositions, and it is crucial that we not declare our interpretations to the world. (See Teaching of the Prophet Joseph Smith, pp. 292-293.) This might be the appropriate place to suggest an overarching, but speculative interpretation that can give us some insight into John's use of language and description. Most biblical scholars suggest that even if John's vision was of a future time, he likely also had immediate concerns in mind. The church of the restoration, for example, was comparable in many respects to the church in his day. In the same way, the evil in his day might be typological of future evil. An article in the May 1973 New Era by Reed Durham gave some tremendous insights and possibilities in this respect. Concerning chapter 13 of Revelation, Reed Durham comments: "John now attempts to describe what to him is the personification of evil in his present day. What is the present anti-Christ that most assuredly will bring about the apostasy in the latter-day (after John's immediate day)? What power is it that is presently destroying the saints and the kingdom that will continue to do so until it is all gone?" a. First beast out of sea (Mediterranean Sea) is Rome and the Roman Empire with all of its parts. b. Leopard = Babylon; bear = Media; lion = Persia. c. Seven heads = seven emperors; ten crowns = the three military generals who ruled Rome for a few months, but who were not Emperors long enough to be considered leaders. d. Head that was wounded as if to death but still lived (see 13: 3, 12, 14; 17: 8, 11) = 'Nero Revivivus.' e. 42 months of apostasy (overcome the saints) = 1260 years! f. 666 -- Nero or Domitian, not the Pope! g. Second beast from the earth (Asia Minor) is the Imperial Cultus or priesthood (real power from devil) that enforced worship of the first beast." (Reed C. Durham, "Revelation, The Plainest Book Ever Written," New Era 1973, pp. 26-27.) The above summary of chapter 13 is not, obviously, the official church position. The Lord has not revealed the correct interpretation, if there is only one. Again, the Durham outline presents possibilities for what John may have had in mind as the enemy of the Church in his day. Of course, the beast is more than the Roman Empire. John's vision grew out of the details of his own historical situation, but its

complete fulfillment will be in our day and in the future. Whatever John saw as the beast in his day was really a type and a shadow of what is to come. Still, as has been the case throughout "Revelation" understanding John's point of reference may make the book more comprehensible and more meaningful for our day. Another reason we have cited Reed Durham's summary, is the fact that it is based on solid scriptural and historical research. His findings are based on a wealth of study from many scholars and students of the scriptures. As we look at each of the verses, we will amplify on his summary, sometimes quoting from Non-LDS scholars. A few Latter-day Saints feel uncomfortable gaining insights from Protestants and Catholics, but it will be helpful to note that Latter-day Saint scriptorians such as Durham have published similar ideas in the church magazines. It is also helpful to note that the comments quoted will be historical and linguistic in nature, and not doctrinal. We will rely on the scriptures, spirit and prophets for our doctrine.
