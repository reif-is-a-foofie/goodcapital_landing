A tower was built of stones, not only to reclaim the rocky soil to more easily facilitate its cultivation, but also to guard and protect the vineyard. It offered an elevated position from which the watchman could see approaching danger (for example, thieves). The vineyard was especially guarded in the harvest season. Remains of thousands of these towers may still be seen in the hill country of Judaea and Samaria.
