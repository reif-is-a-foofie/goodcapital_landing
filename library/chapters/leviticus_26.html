Donaldson Commentary

What are the promises and blessings of the Lord to those who honor the Sabbath day by not shopping on Sunday? The 59th section of the Doctrine and Covenants and the 26th chapter of Leviticus in the Old Testament give similar promises: The fulness of the earth is yours; the land will be blessed with rain and will yield its increase; there will be peace in the land; and God will magnify His faithful people, have respect for them, and establish His covenant with them. (Earl C. Tingey, Ensign, May 1996, 11-12)

One good but mistaken man I know claimed he could get more out of a good book on Sunday than he could get in attending church services, saying that the sermons were hardly up to his standards. But we do not go to Sabbath meetings to be entertained or even simply to be instructed. We go to worship the Lord. It is an individual responsibility, and regardless of what is said from the pulpit, if one wishes to worship the Lord in spirit and in truth, he may do so by attending his meetings, partaking of the sacrament, and contemplating the beauties of the gospel. If the service is a failure to you, you have failed. No one can worship for you; you must do your own waiting upon the Lord.( Spencer W. Kimball, Faith Precedes the Miracle, 270.)

Donaldson Commentary

Spencer W. Kimball has reviewed the promises of the Lord as revealed in this significant chapter: Will we ever turn wholly to God? Fear envelops the world which could be at ease and peace. In God is protection, safety, peace. He has said, "I will fight your battles." But his commitment is on condition of our faithfulness… What a bleak prediction! Yet "These are the statutes and judgments and laws, which the Lord made between him and the children of Israel in Mt. Sinai by the hand of Moses." (Ibid., 26:46.) The Israelites failed to heed the warning. They ignored the prophets. They suffered the fulfillment of every dire prophecy. Do we twentieth century people have reason to think that we can be immune from the same tragic consequences when we ignore the same divine laws? (CR, Oct. 1961, pp. 32-33.) (as quoted by Daniel H. Ludlow, A Companion to Your Study of the Old Testament, 173.)

the carcases of your idols, and my soul shall abhor you.

High places were 6-8 feet high, leveled off, with a stair going up, with one stone representing Baal, with wood posts or trees planted around Baal, in a circle. The posts or trees represented Ashtaroth, idols of female consorts. These were called high places or groves. During spring and planting seasons, they had fertility rites. This involved "vicarious" work for Baal by having sex on the high places with "temple virgins" or prostitutes. This was part of the culture. God literally meant they went whoring after gods of these cultures. These people in Judea had become like Sodom. God put a death penalty on the whole land. Perhaps from God's point of view, he says get them into the spirit world where I can work with them. And you. You are in Pre earth life. God, do you love me? Then don't send me down there, to that culture. I could never gain my salvation under those circumstances. If you love me, you will clean it up. I only have one chance at eternal life. (Robert Norman, 1989 Old Testament Class Notes)
