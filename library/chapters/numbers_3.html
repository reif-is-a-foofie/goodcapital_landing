Donaldson Commentary

NUMBERS - God switches the tribe of Levi with the firstborn. The levitical order is patriarchal. If you are from Aaron, you can be a priest and administer as a priest in the tabernacle. If you are descended from Levi, but not Aaron, then you take care of the tabernacle, carry it, clean it, protect it. The Aaronic order administered in it; Levitical order took care of it. Levites given to Aaron to assist him. Aaron and his sons officiated in the leading offices. Tithing was paid to support the Levites. The Levites didn't get a land, but specific cities. Levites paid a tithe of what they received, to support Aaron. The levitical priesthood was only an appendage to the Aaronic priesthood. It wasn't the same priesthood. House of Israel leaves Sinai, after about a year there. He tells them how to march. Puts Judah out in front, then Reuben, then the Tabernacle, then Reuben and Ephraim way in the back. There had to be feelings. How about all the dust back there, all the time. (Robert Norman, 1989 Old Testament Class Notes)

Donaldson Commentary

cometh nigh (qareb) The key word to the understanding of Numbers 3 is qareb, which should be rendered not as "approach" [cometh nigh in KJV] but in prohibitive contexts, as "encroach" and in permissive contexts, as "qualify" (Numbers 3:6; 18:2; Jeremiah 30:21; Ezekiel 42:13; 44:16)…. The prohibition against encroaching upon the sanctuary is expressed in the formula ha-zar ha-qareb yumat, "a stranger who encroaches shall be put to death." This formula occurs four times, all in the Book of Numbers (1:51; 3:10, 38; 18:7). In the first two instances it is directed to the Levitical cordon outside the sanctuary, and in the latter two, to the priestly cordon within. The uniqueness of this formula is contained in the penalty. Elsewhere crimes against the sanctuary are punishable by God alone, never by man (e.g. improper officiating by priests, in Exodus 28:43; 30:20-21). Only here is a crime against the sanctuary punishable by human agency. The reason lies in the consequence of the act indicated by the verb qareb, "encroach." Illicit contact with sancta produces divine wrath (e.g. Numbers 1:53) or plague (e.g. Numbers 8:19), which is not only liable to strike down the sinner but to engulf the entire community as well (e.g. Numbers 17:11-15, 27-28; 25:9, 18-19: 31:16)… The right to kill with which the sacral cordon is empowered is not to be confused with the legal category of capital punishment whereby death is set a just payment for a particular crime. The action of the guards has nothing to do with justice. The proper category for describing their function goes back to the root purpose of their guarding: mishmeret as a military category. The encroacher, though he be an Israelite, is the enemy who has the power to cause the death of all Israel. (Milgrom, JPS Numbers, 342-343)

And the Lord said to Moses: [Quoted Num. 3:40-41; 44-45.] From that time forth until the coming of our Lord, the Melchizedek Priesthood, which includes the Patriarchal Priesthood, was taken from the tribes of Israel, the one exception being that the prophets received that Priesthood, but always by special ordination. Donaldson Commentary

The first-born of the Egyptians, for whom no lamb as a token of the propitiation was offered, were destroyed. It was through the propitiation and atonement alone that the Israelites were saved, and, under the circumstances they must have perished with the Egyptians, who were doomed, had it not been for the contemplated atonement and propitiation of Christ, of which this was a figure. Hence the Lord claimed those that he saved as righteously belonging to Him, and claiming them as His He demanded their services. … He accepted the tribe of Levi in lieu of the first-born of Israel; and as there were more of the first-born than there were of the Levites, the balance had to be redeemed with money, which was given to Aaron, as the great High Priest and representative of the Aaronic Priesthood, he being also a Levite. [See Numbers 3:50-51.] (John Taylor, Mediation and Atonement, 108.)
