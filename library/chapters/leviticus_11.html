Chickens and goats, which we can eat, are scarcely cleaner by nature than eagles and lions, but the latter are in the class of the unclean." (Wouk, This Is My God, pp. 100-101.)

In regards to Leviticus 11, you'll notice that first are the land creatures, the animals that on the earth

(vv. 1-8); then we find in verses 9-12 the water creatures, those that live under water or in the water, and finally we have the creatures of heaven or the flying creatures.

So we have the same three basic categories found back in Genesis 1 where God created all life that is in the heavens, on earth, and under the waters. Those three categories are all dealt with, and creatures that are in those categories are defined as being either clean or unclean, according to the law given by the Lord. This appears to be a reversal of the curses that stem from the earliest time - even from creation. (see The Clean and Unclean—Part I (Leviticus 11) By: Bob Deffinbaugh)
