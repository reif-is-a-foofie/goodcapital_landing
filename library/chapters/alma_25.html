type Sacred scripture both describes actual events and foreshadows future realities. The Savior clearly taught this dual nature of scripture to the Nephites when he commanded them to search the writings of Isaiah, because this ancient prophet "spake as touching all things concerning my people which are of the house of Israel . . . And all things that he spake have been and shall be , even according to the words which he spake " (3 Nephi 23:1-3; emphasis added). This manner of prophesying is called typology. Typology is a divine means to prepare the minds of God's people for the future. It testifies that God's hand is in history and he directs the affairs of his children. Sacred history, therefore, contains specific events that foreshadow the future.

“So firm a foundation having been laid for the faith of the Nephite people, we find that in every period of their history they retained their reverence for the law of Moses, though disputations sometimes arose, by reason of iniquity, with regard to its symbolism or its saving quality. The apostates, who separated themselves from the Church, occasionally fell into the grievous error of exalting the law above the Gospel, and, whilst maintaining its divine origin, they ignored its typical value and denied that it was a preparatory system leading to a higher, holier and more perfect law; they refused to recognize it as a schoolmaster to bring them to Christ.” (John Taylor, Mediation and Atonement, chapter 14)
