finished As if to reinforce the correlation between the two themes, the account of the construction of the Tabernacle is also laced with phrases and expressions that unmistakably echo the Genesis creation story … This explains why the Tabernacle was finally erected on 'on the first day of the first month,' which is New Year's day, a powerful symbol of the creation of the world, the transformation of chaos into cosmos. The celebration of New Year's day annually inaugurates a new cycle in the life of humankind…. In like manner, therefore, the Tabernacle was conceived to initiate a new era in the life of the community of Israel and the rites that were performed in it thereafter afforded every Israelite the possibility of renewal and moral regeneration. The Tabernacle thus represented, as it were a microcosm in which the macrocosmic universe was reflected. (Sarna, Exploring Exodus, 213-214).
