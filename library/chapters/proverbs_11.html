22[As] a jewel of gold in a swine's snout, [so is] a fair woman which is without discretion.
Donaldson Commentary

Nose jewels were a common adornment for the women of Israel and surrounding cultures, but a jewel in a swine's snout was unthinkable because swine were held in such contempt among the Israelites. The writer of this proverb thus dismisses the value of physical beauty when it is not accompanied with self-control and righteousness. (Old Testament Student Manual: 1 Kings-Malachi, p. 15)
