redeem The Greek equivalent is lutrosis, a ransoming. Paul tells the Saints to prepare for the salvation that has been made available by disengaging from this world-"denying ungodliness and worldly lusts, we should live soberly, righteously, and godly, in this present world"-so that God "might redeem [lutrosetai] us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people." I looked [peculiar] up in a modern dictionary. It is currently defined as “unusual” or “eccentric”; “strange,” “queer,” “odd”; “standing apart from others”; “exclusive” or “unique.” But the term peculiar as used in the scriptures means something quite different. In the Old Testament, the Hebrew term from which peculiar was translated is cgullah, which means “valued property,” or “treasure.” In the New Testament, the Greek term from which peculiar was translated is peripoiesis, which means “possession,” or “an obtaining.” With that understanding, we can see that the scriptural term peculiar does not mean “queer” or “odd” at all.
