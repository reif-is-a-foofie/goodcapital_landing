McConkie●●○○○
To the Father, "the highest of all" (D. & C. 76:70), is ascribed the name, the Highest (Ps. 87:5; Luke 1:32-35, 76; 2:14; 6:35; 19:38), thus signifying that he is exalted above all others in standing, rank, dignity, power, and all things. Of the Father, he who is "the Son of the Highest" (Luke 1:32) proclaimed: "My Father is greater than I." (John 14:28.)McConkie, Mormon Doctrine
