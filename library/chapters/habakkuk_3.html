1 A prayer of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.802">Habakkuk the prophet upon Shigionoth.
2 O <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">LORD, I have heard thy speech, [and] was afraid: O LORD, revive thy work in the midst of the years, in the midst of the years make known; in wrath remember mercy.
3 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">God came from Teman, and the Holy One from mount Paran. Selah. His glory covered the heavens, and the earth was full of his praise.
4 And [his] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">brightness was as the light; he had horns [coming] out of his hand: and there [was] the hiding of his power.
5 Before him went the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjournal_of_discoursesjd_vol_90.277">pestilence, and burning coals went forth at his feet.
6 He <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">stood, and measured the earth: he beheld, and drove asunder the nations; and the everlasting mountains were scattered, the perpetual hills did bow: his ways [are] everlasting.
7 I <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.987">saw the tents of Cushan in affliction: [and] the curtains of the land of Midian did tremble.
8 Was the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">LORD displeased against the rivers? [was] thine anger against the rivers? [was] thy wrath against the sea, that thou didst ride upon thine horses [and] thy chariots of salvation?
9 Thy <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.953">bow was made quite naked, [according] to the oaths of the tribes, [even thy] word. Selah. Thou didst cleave the earth with rivers.
10 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.962">mountains saw thee, [and] they trembled: the overflowing of the water passed by: the deep uttered his voice, [and] lifted up his hands on high.
11 The <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.893">sun [and] moon stood still in their habitation: at the light of thine arrows they went, [and] at the shining of thy glittering spear.
12 Thou didst <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.969">march through the land in indignation, thou didst thresh the heathen in anger.
13 Thou wentest forth for the salvation of thy people, [even] for salvation with <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.983">thine anointed; thou woundedst the head out of the house of the wicked, by discovering the foundation unto the neck. Selah.
14 Thou didst <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.770">strike through with his staves the head of his villages: they came out as a whirlwind to scatter me: their rejoicing [was] as to devour the poor secretly.
15 Thou didst <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.960">walk through the sea with thine horses, [through] the heap of great waters.
16 When I heard, my belly <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.710">trembled; my lips quivered at the voice: rottenness entered into my bones, and I trembled in myself, that I might rest in the day of trouble: when he cometh up unto the people, he will invade them with his troops.
17 Although the fig tree shall not <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.894">blossom, neither [shall] fruit [be] in the vines; the labour of the olive shall fail, and the fields shall yield no meat; the flock shall be cut off from the fold, and [there shall be] no herd in the stalls:
18 Yet I will rejoice in the LORD, I will joy in the God of my salvation.
19 The LORD God [is] my strength, and he will make my <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.962">feet like hinds' [feet], and he will make me to walk upon mine high places. To the chief singer on my stringed instruments.
