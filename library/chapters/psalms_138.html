1[A Psalm] of David. I will praise thee with my whole heart: before the gods will I sing praise unto thee.
Donaldson●●●●●

Before is used here to translate the Hebrew neged, which means "face to face, confronting, opposite." The "gods" were the idols of Babylon; the psalmist in exile defied them by worshipping the living God not only in front of them but also toward God's holy temple. Worship toward the holy temple by Israelites in need, by the absent armies of Israel, by "strangers" in other lands, or by captive Israelites in exile was a mode of worship suggested by Solomon in his dedication of the temple (1 Kings 8:35-50). He prayed that the Lord would hear and bless them all. A confident testimony asserts that the Lord always does answer prayers and will be worshipped by other peoples, including their kings. (Ellis T. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament, 466.)
