It was an ancient practice for the Lord's prophets to take off their garments and shake them as a sign that they were rid of the blood and sins of those to whom they had been sent to testify. (2 Ne. 9:44; Jac. 1:19; 2:2; Mosiah 2:28; Morm. 9:35.) Similar symbolism is used in latter-day revelation: "Cleanse your hearts and your garments, lest the blood of this generation be required at your hands," the Lord says. (D. & C. 112:33; 88:85; 135:5.) (Bruce R. Mc Conkie, Mormon Doctrine, 2d ed., p.304)
