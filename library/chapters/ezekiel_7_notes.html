Donaldson●●●●●

Chapters 8-11 of Ezekiel record one vision with four stages: Chapter 8 - The abominations in the Temple are exposed. Chapter 9 - God's judgment begins. Chapter 10 - God's Glory departs from the Temple. Chapter 11 - God's Glory departs from Jerusalem.

Ezekiel saw four distinct abominations in the Temple that progressed in severity: 1. The Idol of Jealousy worshipped by the people (8:1-6) - This idol cannot be identified with certainty, but is probably the image of Asherah. 2. The animal worship of the elders (8:7-13). 3. The sex corruption of the women (8:14-15) - Tammuz is an ancient Akkadian deity, the husband and brother of Ishtar. He is the god of fertility and rain. He was thought to die early in the fall when the vegetation withered (agrees with the time of Ezekiel's vision) and the wailing of Ishtar renewed him as evidenced by the buds of spring. Licentious fertility festivals celebrated his spring awakening. 4. The sun worship of the priests (8:16-18) - Although Ezekiel does not specifically state that these were priests, it is likely in view of the 24 courses of the priests plus the high priest (1 Chron 23). Sun worship was specifically forbidden by the Mosaic Law (Deut. 4;19) and had been removed under the reigns of Hezekiah and Josiah (2 Kings 23:7; 2 Chron 29:6-7). The priests had turned their backs on God's Temple and were worshipping the created sun.
