Doctrine and Covenants

129

1 There are two kinds of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">beings in heaven, namely: Angels, who are resurrected personages, having bodies of flesh and bones—
2 For instance, Jesus said: <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Handle me and see, for a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have.
3 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Secondly: the spirits of just men made perfect, they who are not resurrected, but inherit the same glory.
4 When a messenger comes saying he has a message from God, offer him your <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_340.936">hand and request him to shake hands with you.
5 If he be an <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">angel he will do so, and you will feel his hand.
6 If he be the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">spirit of a just man made perfect he will come in his glory; for that is the only way he can appear—
7 <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">Ask him to shake hands with you, but he will not move, because it is contrary to the order of heaven for a just man to deceive; but he will still deliver his message.
8 If it be the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">devil as an angel of light, when you ask him to shake hands he will offer you his hand, and you will not feel anything; you may therefore detect him.
9 These are three grand keys whereby <span class="cw" data-tgt="pdonaldsondonaldson_on_moroni_10_341.000">you may know whether any administration is from God.
