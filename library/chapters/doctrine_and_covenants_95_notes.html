Walking in darkness at noon-day. The rebuke here centers in the failure to complete the temple in Kirtland. Again, the temple was to perform two important functions for those called to the ministry: (1) they were to attend the School of the Prophets, where they could be more thoroughly instructed in the doctrines of the kingdom, and (2) they were to be endowed with power from on high. To proceed without such light would be to walk in "darkness at noon-day." (Craig J. Ostler and Joseph Fielding Mc Conkie, Revelations of the Restoration)
