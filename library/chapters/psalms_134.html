Donaldson●●●○○

Extolling Yahweh's mighty power, the psalmist utilizes the structure of Psalm 135 to undo score the contrast between Yahweh and wort1 - less idols. The psalm's opening and closing stanzas are joyous calls to praise; the second and next-to-last stanzas contrast Yahweh's power with the uselessness of the idols; and the central stanza, in the position of emphasis, celebrates Yahweh's power as illustrated in Israel's history. The entire psalm is enclosed by the inclusion "hallelujah!" (David A. Dorsey, The Literary Structure of the Old Testament)
