Donaldson Commentary

Men are commanded to repent, walk uprightly, and sin not. (D. & C. 5:21; 18:31; 46:7.) Parents are to teach their children "to walk uprightly before the Lord." (D. & C. 68:28.) Only the upright will be welcome in Zion (D. & C. 61:16), and all things shall work together for their good. (D. & C. 90:24; 100:15.) Mercy shall be their inheritance (D. & C. 109:1) and salvation their reward. (Ps. 15:1-2; Prov. 28:18; Isa. 33:14-16.) (Bruce R. Mc Conkie, Mormon Doctrine, 2d ed., p.817)

Donaldson Commentary

To backbite is to slander one who is not present. It is a wicked, evil practice, hated of God and fostered by Satan. (Ps. 15:3; Rom. 1:30; 2 Cor. 12:20.) It is the express appointment of the teachers in the Aaronic Priesthood - as part of their mission to do home teaching - to see that there is no backbiting in the Church. (D. & C. 20:54.) (Bruce R. Mc Conkie, Mormon Doctrine, 2d ed., p.69)

Donaldson Commentary

The concern in Psalm 16, as in Psalm 15, is for ultimate salvation and exaltation in the presence of the Lord. The psalmist humbly recognizes that his own goodness is not equal to that of the Lord but hopes it will be like that of the Saints in whom the Lord delights. Idolaters are at the other extreme. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament, p.418-419)
