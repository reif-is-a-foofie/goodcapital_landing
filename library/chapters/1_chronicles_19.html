1 NOW it came to pass after this, that Nahash the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.908">king of the children of Ammon died, and his son reigned in his stead.
2 And <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.790">David said, I will shew kindness unto Hanun the son of Nahash, because his father shewed kindness to me. And David sent messengers to comfort him concerning his father. So the servants of David came into the land of the children of Ammon to Hanun, to comfort him.
3 But the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.972">princes of the children of Ammon said to Hanun, Thinkest thou that David doth honour thy father, that he hath sent comforters unto thee? are not his servants come unto thee for to search, and to overthrow, and to spy out the land?
4 Wherefore <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.686">Hanun took David's servants, and shaved them, and cut off their garments in the midst hard by their buttocks, and sent them away.
5 Then there went [certain], and told David how the men were served. And he sent to meet them: for the men were <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.677">greatly ashamed. And the king said, Tarry at Jericho until your beards be grown, and [then] return.
6 And when the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.696">children of Ammon saw that they had made themselves odious to David, Hanun and the children of Ammon sent a thousand talents of silver to hire them chariots and horsemen out of Mesopotamia, and out of Syriamaachah, and out of Zobah.
7 So they <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">hired thirty and two thousand chariots, and the king of Maachah and his people; who came and pitched before Medeba. And the children of Ammon gathered themselves together from their cities, and came to battle.
8 And when <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">David heard [of it], he sent Joab, and all the host of the mighty men.
9 And the children of Ammon came out, and put the battle in array before the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.634">gate of the city: and the kings that were come [were] by themselves in the field.
10 Now when Joab saw that the battle was set against him before and behind, he <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.768">chose out of all the choice of Israel, and put [them] in array against the Syrians.
11 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.948">rest of the people he delivered unto the hand of Abishai his brother, and they set [themselves] in array against the children of Ammon.
12 And he said, If the Syrians be <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.969">too strong for me, then thou shalt help me: but if the children of Ammon be too strong for thee, then I will help thee.
13 Be of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.959">good courage, and let us behave ourselves valiantly for our people, and for the cities of our God: and let the LORD do [that which is] good in his sight.
14 So <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.993">Joab and the people that [were] with him drew nigh before the Syrians unto the battle; and they fled before him.
15 And when the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.968">children of Ammon saw that the Syrians were fled, they likewise fled before Abishai his brother, and entered into the city. Then Joab came to Jerusalem.
16 And when the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.934">Syrians saw that they were put to the worse before Israel, they sent messengers, and drew forth the Syrians that [were] beyond the river: and Shophach the captain of the host of Hadarezer [went] before them.
17 And it was told <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.971">David; and he gathered all Israel, and passed over Jordan, and came upon them, and set [the battle] in array against them. So when David had put the battle in array against the Syrians, they fought with him.
18 But the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.970">Syrians fled before Israel; and David slew of the Syrians seven thousand [men which fought in] chariots, and forty thousand footmen, and killed Shophach the captain of the host.
19 And when the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.841">servants of Hadarezer saw that they were put to the worse before Israel, they made peace with David, and became his servants: neither would the Syrians help the children of Ammon any more.
