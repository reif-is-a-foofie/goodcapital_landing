How is it that the name "Jesus Christ" appears in the Book of Mormon prior to the time of Christ? by Jeff Lindsay Of course Hebrew speaking people did not use the English term "Jesus Christ". Certainly the revelations given to the Hebrew speaking people early in the Book of Mormon were in Hebrew and used Hebrew forms of names and titles, not Greek or English. The word "diligence" comes from a root that means esteem highly, love, choose, take delight in. Its formal definitions embrace a number of distinct senses. The Oxford English Dictionary defines diligence as "constant and earnest effort to accomplish what is undertaken; persistent application and endeavor; industry; assiduity (constant or close attention to the business at hand); unremitting application." It also has the sense of heedfulness and looking after carefully. Diligence also captures the idea of a "persistent endeavor to please," hence an anxiousness for "speed, dispatch, haste." In the scriptures, a number of Hebrew and Greek words are translated into "diligence." However, most of the uses boil down to two. The first is "to make effort; to be prompt; earnest," and sometimes to be "more prompt and more earnest," and to make haste, or forwardness. The second is "to search out; investigate; crave; inquire; seek after carefully. ... " The word diligence does not appear often in the Old Testament, but there are numerous verses that embody the concept. For example, "And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart" (Jeremiah 29:13). It is in this first sense that Nephi writes that he labors "diligently to write, to persuade our children, and also our brethren, to believe in Christ" (2 Nephi 25:23). Of course, it is also in this sense that we learn and do our duty "in all diligence." It was when Lehi's family was "slothful (see D&C 107:100 for more on slothful) and forgot to exercise their faith and diligence" that they "tarried in the wilderness, or did not travel a direct course" (Alma 37:41-42). In the second sense of diligence as seeking, we are promised that if we "nourish the word ... by (our) faith with great diligence and with patience ... Then ye shall reap the rewards of your faith, and your diligence, and patience, and long-suffering, waiting for the tree to bring forth fruit unto you" (Alma 32:41-43). And, finally, in the Doctrine and Covenants the Lord promises: "seek me diligently and ye shall find me" (D&C 88:63). (Joe Cannon, Deseret News, 9 April 2008)

In chapter 26, Nephi reviews some of the prophecies regarding the coming of Christ among the Nephites, the destruction of the wicked among the Nephites, and the coming forth of the Book of Mormon.
