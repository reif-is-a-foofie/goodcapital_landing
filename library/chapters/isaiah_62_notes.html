The Lord's Vengeance in the Second Coming (63:1-6)

Isaiah has pictured this great day when the Lord shall come with his garments, or apparel, red and glorious, to take vengeance on the ungodly. [Isaiah 63:1-6.] This will be a day of mourning to the wicked, but a day of gladness to all who have kept his commandments. Do not let any one think that this is merely figurative language, it is literal, and as surely as we live that day of wrath will come when the

cup of iniquity is full. We have received a great many warnings. The great day of the Millennium will come in; the wicked will be consumed and peace and righteousness will dwell upon all the face of the earth for one thousand years. (Joseph Fielding Smith, Church History and Modern Revelation 1:191-92.) `The opening verses of Isa. 63 give two important questions and answers about the second coming of Christ. The first question is, "Who comes with dyed garments, . . . traveling in the greatness of his strength?" The answer: "The Righteous One, who is mighty to save" (63:1; see Rev. 19:13, 15; 14:20; D&C 133:46-48). The second question is, "Why are your garments red, as one who has been treading in the winefat?" The answer: "Because I have trodden the winepress alone, and I will trample the wicked and stain my garments with their blood" (63:2-3). The second coming of the Lord will be a "day of vengeance," but it will result in "the year of my redeemed" (63:4). D&C 133:50-53 quotes Isa. 63:3-9 with a few slight changes. Isa. 63:1-9 and 64:1-5 are both quoted in D&C 133 but in reverse order. (Donald W. Parry, Jay A. Parry, and Tina M. Peterson, Understanding Isaiah, p.553)
