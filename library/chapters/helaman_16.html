Types of the Second Coming in Third Nephi by Lee Donaldson Sacred scripture both describes actual events and foreshadows future realities. The Savior clearly taught this dual nature of scripture to the Nephites when he commanded them to search the writings of Isaiah, because this ancient prophet "spake as touching all things concerning my people which are of the house of Israel . . . And all things that he spake have been and shall be , even according to the words which he spake " (3 Nephi 23:1-3; emphasis added). This manner of prophesying is called typology. Typology is a divine means to prepare the minds of God's people for the future. It testifies that God's hand is in history and he directs the affairs of his children. Sacred history, therefore, contains specific events which foreshadow the future. This paper will examine the types in the coming of the Savior to the Nephites and their fulfillment, the antitypes, in the millennial advent of the Lord. We can better understand typology if we comprehend the difference between a symbol and a type. The word symbol "was originally a token, in the form of an object broken in twain, so that the identity could be proved by having the two parts match . . . . This story hides within the word, from the Greek symbolos [which is derived from symballein]" (Shipley, 346). Sym means 'together' and ballein 'to put'. Symbol literally means to put together. A symbol is only valuable when it is put together with its meaning. Therefore, symbolism is, in the broadest sense, the putting together of a physical object with its corresponding concept or idea. Milton Terry pointed out that a symbol has three distinct characteristics. "(1) The names of symbols are to be understood literally; (2) the symbols always denote something essentially different from themselves; and (3) some resemblance, more or less minute, is traceable between the symbol and the thing symbolized" (Terry 356). For instance, the bread of the sacrament is a symbol of the Lord's body (3 Nephi 18:1-7). A symbol is designed "to represent some character, office, or quality" (Terry 336). A symbol is not fixed to a historical time. A type, on the other hand, is tied to a specific time in history and it prefigures a future reality. A type is, as the Savior said, something that "has been and shall be" (3 Nephi 23:3). G.R. Osborne points out that "a symbol deals with the spiritual meaning of terms and a type deals with the deeper concept behind a larger event or institution. Thus types cannot be linked with a symbolic or general expression of spiritual concepts" (Osborne 931). Terry has identified five categories of types; people, ordinances, offices, events, and actions (Terry 338,339). For instance, Adam was a type for Christ (Romans 5:14) and Joseph of Egypt was a type for Joseph Smith (2 Nephi 3:15). The Passover foreshadowed the sacrament. The high priest of ancient Israel entered the holy of holies on the day of atonement for the redemption of Israel to typify when Christ would enter into the heavens having atoned for our sins. In the scriptures, the word "type" and related words such as "typify" only appear in the Book of Mormon (2 Nephi 11:4; Mosiah 13:10; Alma 13:16; 25:10, 15; 33:19; 37:45; Ether 13:6) and the Doctrine and Covenants (D&C 76:70). Although, the Greek word for type, tupos, occurs sixteen times in the New Testament (John 14:26; 20:25; Acts 7:43,44; 23:25; Romans 5:14; 6:17; 1 Cor 10:6, 11; Phillipians 3:17; 1 Thes 1:7; 2 Thes 3:9; 1 Tim 4:12; Titus 2:7; Heb 8:5 1 Peter 5:3) it is never translated as type in the King James Version . It is translated as ensample (5 times), print (2), figure (2), example (2), pattern (2), fashion (1), manner (1), and form (1). Literally, tupos is the mark of a stroke or blow and the print that it makes. A type leaves an exact impression, as opposed to a symbol, which instructs the reader. In fact, our modern typewriter works by striking and making a mark or impression. Thayer notes that types are "the teaching which embodies the sum and substance of religion and represents it to the mind . . . [and is a] pattern of warning [or] ruinous events which serve as admonitions or warnings to others" (Thayer, 632). The future fulfillment of a type is called an antitype (Achtemeier 926-927).

For example, Abraham's sacrifice of Isaac was the type and Heavenly Father's sacrifice of his Only Begotten Son was the antitype. Paul noted that Melchizedek was a type of Christ. Melchizedek, like Christ, was a priest and king whose parentage is not tied to mortal man (Hebrews 7:1- 8:1). Bruce Jorgenson points out a vital underpinning of typology. "Figural [typological] interpretation is not figurative, not merely allegorical, for both figure and fulfillment, both type and antitype, or in Auerbach's word, both 'sign and what it signifies' are regarded as fully historical. When Jesus cites Jonah or Moses' brazen serpent as figures of himself, when Paul cites Adam as a type of Christ, when Jacob in the Book of Mormon cites Abraham's sacrifice as 'a similitude of God and his Only Begotten Son' - none of them denies the historicity of the event or person thus given the added dimension of figural meaning" (Jorgenson, 221). The early apostolic fathers such as Clement of Rome, Ignatius, Polycarp and others viewed the Savior's life as the fulfillment of Old Testament types. However, Philo of Alexandria, in an attempt to marry the Old Testament with Greek philosophy, transformed types into symbols and allegories. This move denied the historical reality of the event. Origen also allegorized sacred history and made it a symbol and not a type. This interpretive framework helped give rise to the great apostasy. Once the church denied the historical reality of scripture, it was a simple matter to change the ordinances and doctrine. A major movement within the Protestant reformation was to again view the scriptures as historical reality with typological meaning. This reform plowed the ground for the restoration of typology as found in the Book of Mormon. The Book of Mormon contained the first scriptural references to typology ever printed in English. Jacob noted that his "soul delighteth in proving unto my people the truth of the coming of Christ; for, for this end hath the law of Moses been given; and all things which have been given of God from the beginning of the world, unto man, are the typifying of him" (2 Nephi 11:4). Benjamin taught that the Law of Moses had a number of types (Mosiah 3:15). Abinadi declared that "all these things [the Law of Moses]were types of things to come" (Mosiah 13:31) and that his own death would be a type of Noah's death (Mosiah 13:10). Alma called the Liahona a type (Alma 37:45). One of the most salient examples of typology in The Book of Mormon is the historical coming of Christ to the Nephites. It typifies the second coming of the Savior. President Benson stated that "in the Book of Mormon we find a pattern for preparing for the Second Coming. A major portion of the book centers on the few decades just prior to Christ's coming to America. By careful study of that time period, we can determine why some were destroyed in the terrible judgments that preceded His coming and what brought others to stand at the temple in the land of Bountiful and thrust their hands into the wounds of His hands and feet" (Benson 6-7). There are at least seven major types in 3 Nephi which foreshadow the Lord's millennial advent. A close look at these events which have been, the types, unlocks a number of insights into events which shall be, the antitypes, at the second coming. First, there were a number of prophecies fulfilled before the Lord's coming to the Nephite saints. The scriptural record indicates the "prophecies of the prophets began to be fulfilled more fully; for there began to be greater signs and greater miracles wrought among the people" (3 Ne 1:4). A number of General Authorities have testified of a parallel fulfillment of prophecy in our day. For instance, President Harold B. Lee noted, "to one who is acquainted with and well versed in the prophetic teachings of the past generations, there should be little question as to the meaning of all that is going on among us today, when it seems as though everything is in turmoil. . . . Before our very eyes we are witnessing the fulfillment of prophecies made by inspired prophets in ages past" (Lee 231). President S. Dilworth Young added that "we see with our own eyes the beginning of the fulfillment of many ancient prophecies and the complete fulfillment of others" (Young 40). Elder Mark E. Petersen's declared that "Moroni fulfilled two biblical prophecies by coming to Joseph Smith" (Petersen 29). Elder Boyd K. Packer testified that the new publication of the scriptures fulfills ancient prophecy. "The stick or record of Judah-the Old Testament and the New Testament-and the stick or record of Ephraim-the Book of Mormon which is another testament of Jesus Christ-are now woven together in such a way that as you pore over one you are drawn to the other; as you learn from one you are enlightened by the other. They are indeed one in our hands. Ezekiel's prophecy now stands fulfilled" ("Scriptures" 53 ). The second type is the persecution of the righteous. There was a core of people within the Nephite society who doubted the prophecies and miracles. They "began to say that the time was past for the words to be fulfilled, which were spoken by Samuel, the

Lamanite. And they began to rejoice over their brethren, saying: Behold the time is past, and the words of Samuel are not fulfilled; therefore, your joy and your faith concerning this thing hath been vain. And it came to pass that they did make a great uproar throughout the land" (3 Nephi 1:5-7). Elder Faust noted that "persecution is not new to the devoted followers of Christ. More recently, however, the anger and venom of our enemies seems to be increasing" (Faust 40). This persecution many times, like the Nephite struggle, comes from within our own society. Ardeth Kapp in a general women's meeting stated "We are warned in the scriptures, 'Yea, and all that will live godly in Christ Jesus shall suffer persecution.' Peer pressure is a form of persecution. Peer pressure is one of the great tests of today" (Kapp 93). This peer pressure takes on the form of persecution as church detractors turn to media to make their attacks on sacred doctrine and priesthood leaders. The key to handling these trials is found in the Nephite record. President Benson, in the same talk where he tied 3 Nephi to the second coming, noted in his next statement that in "the Book of Mormon we find lessons for dealing with persecution" (Benson 7). The Nephites dealt with these trials with faith and patience while waiting on the Lord. They "did watch steadfastly" and their prophet "cried mightily to his God in behalf of his people" (3 Nephi 1:8,11). The Lord answered Nephi's pleadings and protected his people. The means of deliverance was the great sign in heaven which heralded the birth of the Savior. This is the third major type of the Savior's American advent. "And it came to pass that the words which came unto Nephi were fulfilled, according as they had been spoken; for behold, at the going down of the sun there was no darkness; and the people began to be astonished because there was no darkness when the night came" (3 Ne 1:15). This sign saved the lives of the righteous. There will also be a great sign in the heaven when the Son of Man returns. Latter-day revelation informs us that "immediately there shall appear a great sign in heaven, and all people shall see it together" (D&C 88:93). Joseph Smith noted that "the sign of the coming of the Son of Man, which will be as the light of the morning cometh out of the east" (History of the Church 5:337). Brigham Young indicated that "the veil of the covering will be taken from all nations, that all flesh may see his glory together" (Journal of Discourses 2:316). The fourth type was the destruction of the wicked just before the Savior appeared to the Nephites (see 3 Nephi 2-9). This destruction took two different forms. The first destruction of the wicked came from the righteous Nephites as they cast the Gadianton robbers from their midst (3 Nephi 3-5). This intense war with the wicked turned the hearts of the faithful to the Lord insomuch that "there was not a living soul among all the people of the Nephites who did doubt in the least the words of all the holy prophets who had spoken; for they knew that it must needs be that they must be fulfilled" (3 Nephi 5:1). The final destructive stage which consumed the wicked came from the Lord himself. The Lord declared that "many great destructions have I caused to come upon this land, and upon this people, because of their wickedness and their abominations" (3 Nephi 9:12). These great destructions included a "great and terrible destruction in the land northward; for behold, the whole face of the land was changed, because of the tempest and the whirlwinds and the thunderings and the lightnings, and the exceedingly great quaking of the whole earth; And the highways were broken up, and the level roads were spoiled, and many smooth places became rough. And many great and notable cities were sunk, and many were burned, and many were shaken till the buildings thereof had fallen to the earth, and the inhabitants thereof were slain, and the places were left desolate" (3 Nephi 8:12-14). This Nephite cleansing foreshadows the millennial cleansing when the wicked will be destroyed at the advent of the Savior. The Savior himself foretold of "the destruction of the wicked" (JSM 1:31) at his coming. This cleansing will also have two stages. The first will be the "wars and rumors of wars, and the whole earth shall be in commotion" (D&C 45:26). Moroni as he saw our day also prophesied of these wars (Mormon 8:30). The second destructive stage, as in the Nephite experience, will come from divine hands. Joseph Fielding Smith taught that "when the Millennium is ushered in the earth is to pass through a cleansing . . . . All who have lived the telestial law - that is those who are unclean, 'They who are liars, and sorcerers, and whoremongers, and whosoever loves and makes a lie' and who suffer the vengeance of eternal fire' - shall be swept off the face of the earth" (The Way to Perfection 302) The fifth type was the Savior's appearance to the faithful Nephite saints gathered "round about the temple which was in the land Bountiful" (3 Nephi 11:1). Significantly, the Saints were gathered to the holy temple grounds when there was great tribulation and destruction. This

temple site was the place where the Lord chose to appear and show the marks in his hands and feet. This gathering of the faithful to temple grounds foreshadows the appearance of the Lord to his faithful saints at his second coming. Charles Penrose noted that the Lord "will come to the temple prepared for him, and his faithful people will behold his face, hear his voice, and gaze upon his glory. From his own lips they will receive further instructions for the development and beautifying of Zion and for the extension and sure stability of his kingdom" (Penrose 21:582-83). What do we know of this temple? There is a likelihood that this temple will be out-of-doors and not a building if the Nephite type gives us any clues as to the physical characteristic of this temple. The Nephite record indicates that they were "round about the temple" in the land Bountiful (3 Nephi 11:1). Could this millennial advent be an open air appearance to the Saints who are gathered round about the temple in Adam-ondi-Ahman? If this is so, then the Nephite pattern of the Savior appearing to "more righteous part of the people" (3 Nephi 9:12) clearly foreshadows the appearance of the Savior to "all the faithful members of the Church" (Millennial Messiah 579) who will gather to be taught of the Lord. The sixth type was the Savior's face-to-face instruction of the saints. He spent his time among the Nephites teaching them the gospel. His instruction to the ancient American saints was so great that "there cannot be written in this book even a hundredth part of the things which Jesus did truly teach unto the people" (3 Nephi 26:6). Mormon also notes "the eye hath never seen, neither hath the ear heard, before, so great and marvelous things as we saw and heard Jesus speak unto the Father; And no tongue can speak, neither can there be written by any man, neither can the hearts of men conceive so great and marvelous things as we both saw and heard Jesus speak" (3 Nephi 17:16-17). One of the great promises to the righteous is that the Savior will personally teach them during the millennium. Elder Mc Conkie explained when the Savior comes again to the earth "the righteous shall see his face and hear his voice; they shall receive light and truth and wisdom as such fall from his lips . . . . His voice shall again be heard. . . [and the righteous will hear] other sermons as have never entered in the heart of man as Jesus expounds the mysteries of eternity" (Millennial Messiah 653). The seventh type was an era of perfect peace where "there was no contention in the land, because of the love of God which did dwell in the hearts of the people. And there were no envyings, nor strifes, nor tumults, nor whoredoms, nor lyings, nor murders, nor any manner of lasciviousness; and surely there could not be a happier people among all the people who had been created by the hand of God" (4 Nephi 1:15,16). They were a righteous people who had entered into the rest of the Lord and completely cast Satan out of their society. Elder Joseph P. Wirthlin tied the Nephite peace to the peace to be experienced during the millennium. He writes that a "period of peace followed the ministry of the resurrected Jesus among the Nephites . . . [and a] period of perfect peace will come during the Millennium. "Satan shall be bound, that he shall have no place in the hearts of the children of men." As they live the gospel of Jesus Christ, the righteousness of the people will banish Satan from their midst. We look forward to that day of universal peace and justice, when Christ will reign upon the earth" (Wirthlin 38). The Doctrine and Covenants adds "in that day the enmity of man, and the enmity of beasts, yea, the enmity of all flesh, shall cease from before my face" (D&C 101:26). In conclusion, we see that the Lord, through sacred history, shows us things which have been and foreshadows events which shall be. These celestial types of the second coming are carefully woven into the fabric of 3 Nephi. A type is a "pattern of warning" (Thayer, 632) which takes on a more personal note when we understand that types have fulfillment in our own lives. The first type, the voice of warning, came through his prophets. In our own day and time the Lord has called prophets to warn his people. "I the Lord, knowing the calamity which should come upon the inhabitants of the earth, called upon my servant Joseph Smith, Jun., and spake unto him from heaven, and gave him commandments. And also gave commandments to others, that they should proclaim these things unto the world; and all this that it might be fulfilled, which was written by the prophets" (D&C 1:17,18). We, like the ancient Nephites, have the chance to accept or reject the prophetic voice of warning. The second type is our own individual persecution and trials. Lehi saw "a great and spacious building . . . it was filled with people, both old and young, both male and female; and their manner of dress was exceedingly fine and they were in the attitude of mocking and pointing their fingers towards those who had come at and were partaking of the fruit" (1 Nephi 8:26,27). Elder Packer noted "do not expect to see the day when this Church, or those in

it, will be free from resistance, criticism, even persecution. That will never be." (That All May Be Edified 161). Persecution and trials have always been the lot of the righteous. The third type was a great sign in heaven. The Greek word for sign is semeion which can be translated as sign, miracle, wonder, or token. This great sign is a divine witness. Each one of us have signs and miracles in our own lives. These are bright lights in times of peril and darkness. We all have a choice whether we pay attention to this light or wait until the night of darkness when we cannot perform any labors. The fourth type is the destruction of the wicked. Our own cleansing has two stages. The first step is our own effort to forsake and cast the wickedness from our lives. "By this ye may know if a man repenteth of his sins - behold, he will confess them and forsake them" (D&C 58:43). The word forsake comes from the Hebrew word `azab which means to depart from or abandon. Spencer W. Kimball noted that "there is one crucial test of repentance. This is abandonment of the sin" (Kimball 163). The Nephites fought the Gadianton robbers by fortifying "themselves against their enemies; and they did dwell in one land, and in one body" (3 Nephi 3:25). They were able to forsake their enemies by departing from them. The second phase is to have the Lord cleanse us as we follow the light. Mormon parallels the feelings of the Nephite saints at the Bountiful temple with the events that their nation had just physically endured. The voice "did pierce them that did hear to the center, insomuch that there was no part of their frame that it did not cause to quake; yea, it did pierce them to the very soul, and did cause their hearts to burn" (3 Nephi 11:3). These people experienced an internal quaking and burning as their physical surroundings had suffered the effects of quaking and burning. The change of heart which comes from the destruction of wickedness in our own lives permits us to go to the temple to be taught of the Lord. Therefore, the fifth type is when enter into his house and are reconciled to God. Elder Widstoe taught that "it is the great promise that to the temples God will come, and that in them man shall see God. What does this promised communion mean? . . . that the pure in heart who go to the temple, may, there, by the Spirit of God, always have a wonderfully rich communion with God" (Widstoe 12:56). The sixth type is to be taught face-to-face by the Lord. This promise is for all saints who continue to endure in righteousness. The Prophet Joseph Smith stated that "when any man obtains this last Comforter, he will have the personage of Jesus Christ to attend him, or appear unto him from time to time, and even he will manifest the Father unto him, and they will take up their abode with him, and the visions of the heavens will be opened unto him, and the Lord will teach him face to face, and he may have perfect knowledge of the mysteries of the kingdom of God" (Teachings of the Prophet Joseph Smith 151). The final type is to find peace in this life by entering into the rest of the Lord . Elder Mc Conkie wrote that "true saints enter into the rest of the Lord while in this life, and by abiding in the truth, they continue in that blessed state until they rest with the Lord in heaven. (Moro. 7:3; D&C 84:17-25; Matt. 11:28-30; Heb. 3:7-19; 4:1-11.) The rest of the Lord, where mortals are concerned, is to gain a perfect knowledge of the divinity of the great latter-day work. . . . The rest of the Lord, in eternity, is to inherit eternal life, to gain the fulness of the Lord's glory. (D&C 84:24.)" (Mormon Doctrine 633). The real value of studying the types of the second coming is not in satisfying our curiosity, but in understanding how we can stand in the presence of the Lord. These seven types become a pattern for our personal redemption. It would be well to remember President Benson's counsel to carefully study these chapters so "we can determine why some were destroyed in the terrible judgments that preceded His coming and what brought others to stand at the temple in the land of Bountiful and thrust their hands into the wounds of His hands and feet" (Benson 6-7).
