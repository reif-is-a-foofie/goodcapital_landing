4 [With] <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">three rows of great stones, and a row of new timber: and let the expenses be given out of the king's house:

6 Now [therefore], <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.694">Tatnai, governor beyond the river, Shetharboznai, and your companions the Apharsachites, which [are] beyond the river, be ye far from thence:

10 That they may offer sacrifices of <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.943">sweet savours unto the God of heaven, and pray for the life of the king, and of his sons.
11 Also I have made a decree, that whosoever shall alter this word, let timber be <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.876">pulled down from his house, and being set up, let him be hanged thereon; and let his house be made a dunghill for this.

13 Then Tatnai, <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.716">governor on this side the river, Shetharboznai, and their companions, according to that which Darius the king had sent, so they did speedily.

15 And this <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.943">house was finished on the third day of the month Adar, which was in the sixth year of the reign of Darius the king.

17 And offered at the dedication of this house of God an hundred <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.995">bullocks, two hundred rams, four hundred lambs; and for a sin offering for all Israel, twelve he goats, according to the number of the tribes of Israel.
18 And they set the priests in their divisions, and the Levites in their <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.717">courses, for the service of God, which [is] at Jerusalem; as it is written in the book of Moses.
19 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.925">children of the captivity kept the passover upon the fourteenth [day] of the first month.
20 For the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.583">priests and the Levites were purified together, all of them [were] pure, and killed the passover for all the children of the captivity, and for their brethren the priests, and for themselves.
21 And the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.883">children of Israel, which were come again out of captivity, and all such as had separated themselves unto them from the filthiness of the heathen of the land, to seek the LORD God of Israel, did eat,
22 And kept the <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.968">feast of unleavened bread seven days with joy: for the LORD had made them joyful, and turned the heart of the king of Assyria unto them, to strengthen their hands in the work of the house of God, the God of Israel.
