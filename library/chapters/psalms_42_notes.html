The striking simile with which Psalm 42 opens expresses the yearning of a soul to return to the temple and to the presence of God. It begins a collection of worship songs identified with "the sons of Korah." Korah, a Levite descended through Kohath, died in a cataclysm following a rebellion, but his children did not die (Num. 16; 26:11). Their descendants were singers in the temple (2 Chr. 20:19; BD, "Korah"). Ten psalms are similarly identified: 42, 44-49, 85, 87, 88. (Ellis T. Rasmussen, A Latter-day Saint Commentary on the Old Testament, 428.)
