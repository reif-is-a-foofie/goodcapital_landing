6 [Is] not this the fast that I have chosen? to <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18671.000">loose the bands of wickedness, to undo the heavy burdens, and to let the oppressed go free, and that ye break every yoke?

8 Then shall thy <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.922">light break forth as the morning, and thine health shall spring forth speedily: and thy righteousness shall go before thee; the glory of the LORD shall be thy rereward.
9 Then shalt thou <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.949">call, and the LORD shall answer; thou shalt cry, and he shall say, Here I [am]. If thou take away from the midst of thee the yoke, the putting forth of the finger, and speaking vanity;
10 And [if] thou <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.978">draw out thy soul to the hungry, and satisfy the afflicted soul; then shall thy light rise in obscurity, and thy darkness [be] as the noonday:

13 If thou <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.950">turn away thy foot from the sabbath, [from] doing thy pleasure on my holy day; and call the sabbath a delight, the holy of the LORD, honourable; and shalt honour him, not doing thine own ways, nor finding thine own pleasure, nor speaking [thine own] words:
14 Then shalt thou <span class="cw" data-tgt="pjstjst__inspired_version_18670.701">delight thyself in the LORD; and I will cause thee to ride upon the high places of the earth, and feed thee with the heritage of Jacob thy father: for the mouth of the LORD hath spoken [it].
