6, 7. and laid them by heaps--The contributions began to be sent in shortly after the celebration of the passover, which had taken place in the middle of the second month. Some time would elapse before the king's order reached all parts of the kingdom. The wheat harvest occurred in the third month, so that the sheaves of that grain, being presented before any other, formed "the foundation," an under-layer in the corn stores of the temple. The first-fruits of their land produce which were successively sent in all the summer till the close of the fruit and vintage season, that is, the seventh month, continued to raise heap upon heap. (Jamieson-Faussett-Brown Commentary)

2Ch 31:11-19. HEZEKIAH APPOINTS OFFICERS TO DISPOSE OF THE TITHES

11-18. Hezekiah commanded to prepare chambers in the house of the Lord--storehouses, granaries, or cellars; either the old ones, which had been allowed through neglect to fall into decay, were to be repaired, or additional ones built. Private individuals brought their own first-fruits to the temple; but the tithes were levied by the Levites, who kept a faithful account of them in their several places of abode and transmitted the allotted proportion to the priests. Officers were appointed to distribute equal rations to all in the cities of the priests who, from age or other reasons, could not repair to the temple. With the exception of children under three years of age--an exception made probably from their being considered too young to receive solid food--lists were kept of the number and age of every male; of priests according to their fathers' house, and Levites from twenty years (see Nu 4:3; 28:24; 1Ch 23:24). But, besides, provision was also made for their wives, daughters, and servants (Jamieson-Faussett-Brown Commentary)

When the blessings of the priesthood were restored at the time of Moses-through Moses-the ordinances of the priesthood were also restored. These ordinances we refer to as the works done in the flesh, which means the works done here upon this earth. These are the priesthood works. In order to do this, a portable temple was constructed. Brothers and sisters, this was only for the living. There was as yet no work for the dead. There couldn't be, for no atonement had as yet been made. The door had not yet been opened. Even in the more permanent temple of Solomon and the temple that followed after that, only work for the living was done. The priesthood ordinances were given to a select few, those who had not rejected the Melchizedek Priesthood, for most of them had rejected the Melchizedek Priesthood and its accompanying blessings and ordinances. There were a select few men and women who kept the covenants of God, who, by special permission were given the privilege of having these ordinances performed while they were living in the flesh, who were judged worthy to receive these blessings; and a genealogy was kept of the sons and daughters of God. This is referred to in the Bible and you will find it in 2 Chr. 31:16-19. (Theodore M. Burton, August 8, 1966, BYU Speeches of the Year, 1966, p.6)
