A Place Without a Thief. — At Berezov, a thief is almost an unheard-of occurrence. Not one of the residents, either Ostiak or Russian, has ever been found guilty of such an offence. Whenever a theft has been committed, the guilty party has, on investigation, invariably proved to be a stranger — one who, fostered amidst a higher civilization than the natives of this remote region can boast, came here to disgrace it. Only such persons as have been banished from other parts have ever been detected in this crime, and they are not many in number.
