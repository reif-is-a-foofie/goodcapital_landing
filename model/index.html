The question is simple: if the assumptions are true, does the business work? LP capital is committed, called only when net deployment requires it, retained spread arrives in cash at settlement, profit is earned over time, principal is recycled, and GP carry only mints from annual earned profit above the LP hurdle.

Scope And Accounting
Product In Scope
This version models one product only: short-duration Australian private credit with prepaid interest deducted at settlement. Progressive-draw development facilities, line fees, and construction drawdowns are intentionally out of scope for this phase.
Revenue Recognition
Three layers stay separate in the code and on the page: cash collected upfront, unearned retained spread reserve, and earned spread recognized monthly. Changing the term changes the pace of earnings recognition automatically.
Carry Rule
GP carry crystallizes once per year. The model uses annual earned profit above the LP hurdle, converts that carry into new GP units, and tracks the resulting ownership dilution and mark-to-model value.

Version 1 Scope Disclosure
This first feasibility model assumes no defaults, no extensions, no losses, no recovery lags, no liquidity events, and no progressive-draw development facilities. It models one prepaid short-duration credit product only. Prepaid retained spread is collected in cash at settlement, held in an unearned reserve, and recognized monthly over the stated term. This is an operating engine for proving the base-case economics, not a full underwriting model.
