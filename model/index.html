This simplified build assumes prepaid interest at settlement. Borrowers are charged 25.0%, the fund retains 17.0%, and that retained spread is deducted upfront from settlement proceeds. For NAV and carry, that prepaid spread is recognized ratably over the 12-month loan term.
How Capital Moves
1. Capital Calls
LPs commit a maximum amount. If capital calls are on, the model only calls capital when net cash needed for new loans exceeds cash already on hand. If capital calls are off, the full commitment is funded at the start.
2. Loan Settlement
Each month the fund targets a fixed amount of gross commitments. Because spread is prepaid and retained at closing, the actual cash required is less than the gross loan amount.
3. Profit Recognition
Prepaid spread improves cash immediately, but it becomes profit only as time passes. That is why the model shows both cash and unearned prepaid spread separately.
4. GP Units
At each year-end, GP carry is minted in units based on annual earned profit above the LP preferred return. The GP receives value without taking cash out of the fund.
