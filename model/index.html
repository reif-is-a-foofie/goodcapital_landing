FAQ
How Is Loan Volume Modeled?
Monthly origination volume is modeled as the lesser of pipeline capacity and deployable capital. In plain English: the fund can only make as many loans as the market can absorb and as its available equity, leverage, repayments, retained profits, and remaining capital calls can support.
Borrower Rate Vs. Fund Take
Borrower Rate is the gross rate the developer pays on the loan. Fund Take is the portion the fund retains after paying origination partners and other loan economics. That retained spread is what drives NAV growth.
How Do GP Units Work?
GP units are minted only when carry crystallizes. In this model, carry is tested against the LP hurdle over time and crystallizes annually instead of month by month, so the GP does not take a large ownership jump at fund launch.
What Drives The Chart?
NAV shows total fund value, Loans Outstanding shows active principal still out in the field, Cash shows liquidity on hand, and the brown bars show new loan volume per month so you can judge whether the market would need to absorb more lending as the strategy scales.
